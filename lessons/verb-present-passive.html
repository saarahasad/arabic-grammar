Present Passive

الْمُضَارِعُ الْمَجْهُولُ

The passive mudāriʿ uses the pattern يُفْعَلُ (and the same prefixes as the active mudāriʿ: يَ, تَ, etc., with passive stem shapes). The meaning is present or future “is / are / will be done” without naming the doer.

Only verbs that can be passive in the past (transitive مُتَعَدٍّ) have a passive mudāriʿ in the usual way.

Examples (passive mudāriʿ in context)

إِلَيْهِ يُرْجَعُ الْأَمْرُ كُلُّهُ — To Him every affair is returned (11:123)
لَا تَظْلِمُونَ وَلَا تُظْلَمُونَ — You wrong not and are not wronged… (2:279)
قُل لَّا تُسْأَلُونَ عَمَّا أَجْرَمْنَا — Say, “You will not be questioned about what we committed.” (34:25)
وَلَا نُسْأَلُ عَمَّا تَعْمَلُونَ — Nor will we be questioned about what you do (34:25)
وَيَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ لَا يُنصَرُونَ — And on the Day of Resurrection they will not be helped (28:41)
ذَٰلِكَ أَدْنَىٰ أَن يُعْرَفْنَ — That is more suitable that they be known (33:59)
