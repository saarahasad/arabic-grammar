Attached Pronouns with Nouns

ضَمَائِر مُتَّصِلَة مَعَ الْأَسْمَاء

When attached to a noun, the pronoun indicates possession. The noun precedes the pronoun; in translation, the pronoun (possessive) comes first.

Example: كِتَاب (book)

Pronoun suffix (possessive)

PersonSingularDualPlural
3rd Masc.كِتَابُهُ (His book)كِتَابُهُمَاكِتَابُهُمْ
3rd Fem.كِتَابُهَا (Her book)كِتَابُهُمَاكِتَابُهُنَّ
2nd Masc.كِتَابُكَ (Your book)كِتَابُكُمَاكِتَابُكُمْ
2nd Fem.كِتَابُكِكِتَابُكُمَاكِتَابُكُنَّ
1stكِتَابِي (My book)كِتَابُنَا (Our book)
Rule: For 1st person singular, the noun takes ـِي and the last letter of the noun has kasra (ـِ) before the attachment, e.g. كِتَابِي.

Quranic Examples

رَبُّهُ — his Lord (2:131)
رَبُّكَ — your Lord (2:30)
قُلُوبُنَا — our hearts (2:88)
نِعْمَتِي — My favor (2:47)
