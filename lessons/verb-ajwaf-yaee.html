Hollow Verb (Middle Yāʾ)

الْفِعْلُ الْأَجْوَفُ الْيَائِيُ

When the middle root letter is ي, the past tense often shows a long ا (as in بَاعَ from underlying بَيَعَ “to sell”). The مُضَارِع usually spells that weak letter as ي with كَسْرَة on the first root consonant (يَبِيعُ).

—بَاعَبَاعَابَاعُوا

—بِعْتَبِعْتُمَابِعْتُمْ
2nd feminine الْحَاضِرَةُ الْمُؤَنَّثُ
—بِعْتِبِعْتُمَابِعْتُنَّ
1st common الْمُتَكَلِّمُ

From the third-person feminine plural row downward, the middle ا drops and the vowel on the first root letter often shifts from فَتْحَة to كَسْرَة (بِعْنَ, بِعْتَ, …).

In the mudāriʿ, the middle ا of the past is usually realized as ي with كَسْرَة on the first root letter; in feminine plurals the ي may also drop (يَبْعْنَ, تَبْعْنَ).

Imperative (الْأَمْرُ)

SingularDualPlural
Masc.بِعْبِيعَابِيعُوا
Fem.بِيعِيبِيعَابِعْنَ
In the imperative, ي is dropped only in the masculine singular (بِعْ) and in the feminine plural (بِعْنَ).

Summary rules (textbook)

  1. In the past, from third-person feminine plural through the end of the table, the alif drops and the vowel on the first root letter often becomes kasra.
  2. In the mudāriʿ, the alif is replaced by yāʾ (with kasra on the first root letter), except in feminine plurals where yāʾ may also drop.
  3. In the imperative, yāʾ drops in masculine singular and feminine plural only.

فَلَمَّا زَاغُوا أَزَاغَ اللَّهُ قُلُوبَهُمْ — When they went wrong, Allah let their hearts go wrong. (61:5)
وَأَوْفُوا الْكَيْلَ إِذَا كِلْتُمْ — Give full measure when you measure. (17:35)

وَيَزِيدُ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ اهْتَدَوْا هُدًى — Allah increases guidance for those who seek guidance. (19:76)
إِنَّهُمْ يَكِيدُونَ كَيْدًا — Indeed they are plotting a scheme. (86:15)
فَلَا تَمِيلُوا كُلَّ الْمَيْلِ — Do not incline altogether… (4:129)
قُلْ سِيرُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ — Say, “Travel in the earth.” (6:11)
إِنَّ الَّذِينَ يُحِبُّونَ أَن تَشِيعَ الْفَاحِشَةُ — Those who love that scandal be spread… (24:19)
