Jazm

الْجَزْمُ

Jazm (الْجَزْمُ) is the jussive grammatical state. The sign is often sukūn (السُّكُونُ) on the last letter of the verb.

A verb in this state is called مَجْزُومٌ (majzūm).

لَمْ أَذْهَبْlam adhhab — I did not go (jazm on the verb after لَمْ)

Where it appears

  • Only on the present/future verb (الْفِعْلُ الْمُضَارِعُ), after particles like لَمْ, لَمَّا, and in prohibition with لَا.
  • Nouns are not majzūm.
لَمْ يَذْهَبْ — He did not go
