Verbs with Hamza (ء)

الْفِعْلُ الْمَهْمُوزُ

A hamzated verb (الْفِعْلُ الْمَهْمُوزُ) has the letter ء (hamza) as one of its three root consonants. An alif with no vowel marks can act as a long vowel; an alif with any حَرَكَة is read as hamza (a consonant). Hamza may stand at the first, second, or third position of the root.

Quranic samples — first root letter hamza

حَتَّىٰ إِذَا جَاءَ أَمْرُنَا — Until when Our command came… (11:40)

وَأُمِرْتُ أَنْ أَكُونَ مِنَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ — And I was commanded to be of the believers (10:104)
فَافْعَلُوا مَا تُؤْمَرُونَ — Then do what you are commanded (2:68)
وَأْمُرْ أَهْلَكَ بِالصَّلَاةِ — And command your family to prayer (20:132)
