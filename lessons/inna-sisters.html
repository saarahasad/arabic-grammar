Inna & its sisters

إِنَّ وَ أَخَوَاتُهَا

Inna and its sisters (إِنَّ وَأَخَوَاتُهَا) are particles (حُرُوف) that enter a nominal sentence (جُمْلَةٌ إِسْمِيَّةٌ) and change its iʿrāb. Each particle has its own shade of meaning; grammatically they share one pattern: the former مُبْتَدَأ becomes the ism of the particle (e.g. اسْمُ إِنَّ) and is manṣūb; the former خَبَر becomes the khabar of the particle (e.g. خَبَرُ إِنَّ) and stays marfūʿ.

The six sisters

These six particles are often learned as a set. Memorize the list, then learn how each one affects الْمُبْتَدَأ and الْخَبَر below.

ParticleMeaningNotesExample
إِنَّIndeed / verilyEmphasis (تَوْكِيد); often sentence-initial or after قَالَإِنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ
أَنَّThat (indeed)After many verbs of saying/knowing/etc. (عَلِمْتُ أَنَّ …)عَلِمْتُ أَنَّ خَالِدًا مَرِيضٌ
كَأَنَّAs ifSimilitude (تَشْبِيه) or sometimes suppositionكَأَنَّ الْمَسْجِدَ مَدْرَسَةٌ
لَكِنَّBut / howeverCorrection or contrast (اسْتِدْرَاك)مُحَمَّدٌ مُجْتَهِدٌ لَكِنَّ حَامِدًا كَسْلَانُ
لَعَلَّPerhaps / I hopeHope or expectation (تَرَجِّي)لَعَلَّ الْمُدَرِّسَ فِي غُرْفَتِهِ
لَيْتَIf only / would thatWish, often for the impossible or very difficult (تَمَنِّي)لَيْتَ الشَّبَابَ يَعُودُ

إِنَّ vs أَنَّ (classical rule of thumb)

  • إِنَّ — Often begins the sentence, or comes after قَالَ (“he said”) and verbs like it.
  • أَنَّ — Usually after other verbs, for example: عَلِمْتُ (I knew), سَمِعْتُ (I heard), رَأَيْتُ (I saw), and similar.
  • Remember: The surrounding verb and context decide which form appears.

The rule: الْمُبْتَدَأ and الْخَبَر

A nominal sentence has two main parts: the مُبْتَدَأ (topic / “subject”) and the خَبَر (comment / predicate). In a plain nominal sentence, both are usually marfūʿ (مَرْفُوع).

When إِنَّ or one of its sisters enters that sentence, the rule is:

هِيَ تَنْصِبُ الْإِسْمَ وَتَرْفَعُ الْخَبَرَ.

In other words: the word that was the مُبْتَدَأ becomes the اسْمُ of the particle and takes naṣb (usually a فَتْحَة on the last letter, or the appropriate naṣb sign); the خَبَر becomes خَبَرُ the particle and stays marfūʿ (often ضَمَّة or تَنْوِينٌ with ضَمَّة).

Before and after إِنَّ

Plain الْوَلَدُ صَالِحٌ—The boy is righteous.
إِنَّ إِنَّ الْوَلَدَ صَالِحٌ—Indeed, the boy is righteous.
  • manṣūb الْوَلَدُ → الْوَلَدَ: اسْمُ إِنَّ (e.g. ـَ on the last letter).
  • marfūʿ صَالِحٌ: خَبَرُ إِنَّ (e.g. ـُن).

This is the opposite of كَانَ and its sisters: there اسْمُ كَانَ is marfūʿ and خَبَرُ كَانَ is manṣūb. See the lesson Kaana & its sisters.

إِنَّ with detached pronouns

The particle إِنَّ can combine with a detached pronoun (ضَمِيرٌ مُنْفَصِلٌ) to form a single word: إِنَّ + pronoun → attached form (same pattern as other إِنَّ sisters that take this construction). Read each line as: Inna + pronoun = combined.

المُفْرَد singular

إِنَّ+هُوَ=إِنَّهُ
إِنَّ+هِيَ=إِنَّهَا
إِنَّ+أَنْتَ=إِنَّكَ
إِنَّ+أَنْتِ=إِنَّكِ
إِنَّ+أَنَا=إِنَّنِي/إِنِّي

الْجَمْعُ plural

إِنَّ+هُمْ=إِنَّهُمْ
إِنَّ+هُنَّ=إِنَّهُنَّ
إِنَّ+أَنْتُمْ=إِنَّكُمْ
إِنَّ+أَنْتُنَّ=إِنَّكُنَّ
إِنَّ+نَحْنُ=إِنَّنَا/إِنَّا

Glosses: إِنَّهُ — “indeed he/it”; إِنَّهَا — “indeed she/it”; إِنَّكَ / إِنَّكِ — “indeed you” (masc./fem.); إِنَّنِي / إِنِّي — “indeed I”; plural forms: “indeed they,” “indeed you all,” “indeed we.” Two forms are listed where both are common in Qurʾānic and classical usage.

Qurʾānic references for لَعَلَّ

Like the other sisters, لَعَلَّ enters a nominal sentence: اسْمُ لَعَلَّ is manṣūb and خَبَرُ لَعَلَّ is marfūʿ. The Arabic column shows the لَعَلَّ clause (or the operative phrase in context).

1 · لَعَلَّكُمْ تَتَّقُونَ — “that you may become righteous / fear Allāh”

2:21 O mankind, worship your Lord, who created you and those before you… لَعَلَّكُمْ تَتَّقُونَ لَعَلَّكُمْ (particle + ـكُمْ) تَتَّقُونَ (jumlah fiʿliyya)
2:183 O you who have believed, prescribed for you is fasting… لَعَلَّكُمْ تَتَّقُونَ لَعَلَّكُمْ (particle + ـكُمْ) تَتَّقُونَ (jumlah fiʿliyya)

Key Qurʾānic examples of لَيْتَ

لَيْتَ expresses regret or a strong wish (تَمَنِّي); grammatically it follows the same ism / khabar pattern as the other sisters: اسْمُ لَيْتَ (manṣūb) and خَبَرُ لَيْتَ (marfūʿ). The Arabic column gives the operative لَيْتَ clause (sometimes after يَا).

Ref. Theme / gloss Arabic اسْمُ لَيْتَ (manṣūb) خَبَرُ لَيْتَ (marfūʿ)
78:40 Regret of the disbeliever: “If only I were dust!” يَا لَيْتَنِي كُنْتُ تُرَابًا لَيْتَنِي (particle + ـنِي) كُنْتُ تُرَابًا (jumlah fiʿliyya)
25:28 Regret over bad companionship: “If only I had not taken so-and-so as a close friend!” يَا وَيْلَتَىٰ لَيْتَنِي لَمْ أَتَّخِذْ فُلَانًا خَلِيلًا لَيْتَنِي (particle + ـنِي) لَمْ أَتَّخِذْ فُلَانًا خَلِيلًا (jumlah fiʿliyya)
33:66 Wish they had obeyed Allah and the Messenger يَا لَيْتَنَا أَطَعْنَا اللَّهَ وَأَطَعْنَا الرَّسُولَ لَيْتَنَا (particle + ـنَا) أَطَعْنَا اللَّهَ وَأَطَعْنَا الرَّسُولَ (jumlah fiʿliyya)

28:79 Longing for wealth like Qārūn’s يَا لَيْتَ لَنَا مِثْلَ مَا أُوتِيَ قَارُونُ مِثْلَ مَا أُوتِيَ قَارُونُ (with لَيْتَ لَنَا) — (no separate marfūʿ predicate in many analyses)

Qurʾānic references for لَٰكِنَّ

لَٰكِنَّ gives correction or contrast (اسْتِدْرَاك). Each row is analyzed as اسْمُ لَٰكِنَّ (manṣūb) and خَبَرُ لَٰكِنَّ (marfūʿ) where the particle actually occurs. 2:12 is a contrast example: the first half is إِنَّ; the second half is وَلَٰكِن + لَا (not the sister لَٰكِنَّ) — see the notes on that row.

Ref. Gloss Arabic اسْمُ لَٰكِنَّ (manṣūb) خَبَرُ لَٰكِنَّ (marfūʿ)
