Magnified nouns — the five special nouns

الْأَسْمَاءُ الْمُكَبَّرَة · الْأَسْمَاءُ الْخَمْسَة

This chapter · الْأَسْمَاءُ الْخَمْسَة

Five special nouns

These five nouns are often called magnified (مُكَبَّرَة) — الْأَسْمَاءُ الْمُكَبَّرَة — or the five (الْخَمْسَة) when they behave as مُضَاف (first part of an iḍāfah).

The five nouns

أَبٌ أَخٌ ذُو حَمٌ فَمٌ

When one of these is the first part of an iḍāfah (مُضَاف), it takes wāw (rafʿ), alif (naṣb), or yāʾ (jarr) before the possessor — not ordinary triptote endings. Read this chapter in order: big picture first, then each noun, then the special “my” rule at the end.

1Overview — the five as مُضَاف

Labels used in many charts: مَرْفُوعٌ مُضَاف (wāw), مَنْصُوبٌ مُضَاف (alif), مَجْرُورٌ مُضَاف (yāʾ).

Qurʾān and these patterns: أَب, أَخ, and ذُو appear frequently in the مُضَاف shapes above. By contrast, the sequences حَمُو / حَمَا / حَمِي and فُو / فَا / فِي (the “five-noun” declension for حَم and فَم) are not found in the Qurʾān — see the حَم and فَم sections below. فَم itself occurs (e.g. فَاهُ), but not as فُو/فَا/فِي in this construction.

2أَبٌ — father

As مُضَاف: أَبُو / أَبَا / أَبِي (rafʿ / naṣb / jarr).

وَأَبُونَا شَيْخٌ كَبِيرٌ — Our father is a very old man (28:23) — أَبُو marfūʿ
وَوَرِثَهُ أَبَوَاهُ — And his parents are his heirs (4:11)
وَرَفَعَ أَبَوَيْهِ عَلَى الْعَرْشِ — And he raised his parents upon the Throne (12:100)
وَجَاءُوا أَبَاهُمْ عِشَاءً يَبْكُونَ — They came to their father at night, weeping (12:16)
إِذْ قَالَ يُوسُفُ لِأَبِيهِ — When Joseph said to his father (12:4)

3أَخٌ — brother

As مُضَاف: أَخُو / أَخَا / أَخِي.

وَاذْكُرْ أَخَا عَادٍ — Mention the brother of ʿĀd (46:21)
كَيْفَ يُوَارِي سَوْءَةَ أَخِيهِ — How can he hide his brother’s shame? (5:31)
إِذْ قَالَ لَهُمْ أَخُوهُمْ — When their brother said to them (26:106)

4ذُو / ذَاتُ — possessor (“dhū / dhāt”)

Most special among the five: ذُو always functions as مُضَاف — it denotes possession or “having” (e.g. possessor of wealth, of knowledge). It does not stand alone like the others can in their indefinite forms.

ذُو — مَعْنَاهَا (صَاحِبٌ)، وَذُو دَائِمًا مُضَافٌ وَالْاسْمُ الَّذِي يَلِيهِ مَجْرُورٌ بِالْإِضَافَةِ.

In English: “possessor” or “owner.” The following noun is مَجْرُور (genitive) as مُضَاف إِلَيْهِ.

إِنَّ اللَّهَ هُوَ الرَّزَّاقُ ذُو الْقُوَّةِ الْمَتِينِ — Indeed Allah is the All-Provider, Possessor of power, the Mighty (51:58)

Sample sentences

هٰذَا الطَّالِبُ ذُو عِلْمٍThis student (masc. sing.) has / is endowed with knowledge.
هٰؤُلَاءِ الطُّلَّابُ ذَوُو عِلْمٍThese students (masc. pl.) are possessors of knowledge.
هٰذِهِ الطَّالِبَةُ ذَاتُ عِلْمٍThis student (fem. sing.) has knowledge.
هٰؤُلَاءِ الطَّالِبَاتُ ذَوَاتُ عِلْمٍThese students (fem. pl.) are possessors of knowledge.

More Qurʾānic samples — ذُو / ذَات / أُولُو

يَحْكُمُ بِهِ ذَوَا عَدْلٍ مِنكُمْ — …as judged by two just men among you (5:95)
ذَوَاتَا أَفْنَانٍ — Having two kinds (of shade) (55:48)
وَلَوْ كَانَ ذَا قُرْبَىٰ — Even if a near relative were concerned (5:106)
وَبِالْوَالِدَيْنِ إِحْسَانًا وَذِي الْقُرْبَىٰ — …and to parents do good, and to near relatives (2:83)
وَحَمَلْنَاهُ عَلَىٰ ذَاتِ أَلْوَاحٍ وَدُسُرٍ — We carried him on a structure of planks and nails (54:13)
وَأُولُو الْأَرْحَامِ بَعْضُهُمْ أَوْلَىٰ بِبَعْضٍ — Blood relatives are more entitled to one another (33:6)
وَيَبْقَىٰ وَجْهُ رَبِّكَ ذُو الْجَلَالِ وَالْإِكْرَامِ — And there will remain the Face of your Lord, Owner of Majesty and Bounty (55:27)
تَبَارَكَ اسْمُ رَبِّكَ ذِي الْجَلَالِ وَالْإِكْرَامِ — Blessed be the name of your Lord, Possessor of Majesty and Bounty (55:78)
وَاللَّهُ ذُو الْفَضْلِ الْعَظِيمِ — Allah is possessor of great bounty (2:105)

5حَمٌ — in-law (father/kin of spouse)

As مُضَاف, the textbook shapes are حَمُو / حَمَا / حَمِي.

✗ Not in the Qurʾān in these مُضَاف endings (حَمُو / حَمَا / حَمِي). The word may be discussed in classical grammar for completeness.

6فَمٌ — mouth

As مُضَاف, the textbook shapes are فُو / فَا / فِي.

✗ Not in the Qurʾān in these مُضَاف endings (فُو / فَا / فِي). The Qurʾān uses فَم in other constructions (e.g. with pronoun suffixes).

إِلَى الْمَاءِ لِيَبْلُغَ فَاهُ — …to the water, that it might reach his mouth (13:14)

7Special case: fixed ي “my” (أَبِي، أَخِي، فَمِي)

When أَب, أَخ, or فَم is مُضَاف to my (ـِي), the form is fixed as أَبِي, أَخِي, فَمِي in every case.

إِنَّ أَبِي يَدْعُوكَ — Indeed my father invites you (28:25)
وَاغْفِرْ لِأَبِي — And forgive my father (26:86)
فَأَلْقُوهُ عَلَىٰ وَجْهِ أَبِي — Cast it upon my father’s face (12:93)
إِنَّ هَذَا أَخِي — This is my brother (38:23)
فَأُوَارِيَ سَوْءَةَ أَخِي — That I might hide my brother’s shame (5:31)

فَمِي does not occur in the Qurʾān.
