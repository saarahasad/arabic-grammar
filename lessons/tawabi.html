The Followers (At-Tawābiʿ)

التوابع

At-tawābiʿ (التوابع) are words that follow a preceding word — the matbūʿ (الْمَتْبُوع) — and share its grammatical case (iʿrāb). In one formulation: a noun that follows what is before it in iʿrāb and does not carry an independent case of its own in that relation.

Singular: التابع — plural: التوابع.

Four main types

  1. An-naʿt (النَّعْت) — the adjective / attribute. Open the Naʿt node under Noun for agreement rules and examples.
  2. Al-ʿaṭf (الْعَطْف) — conjunction (coordinated noun or phrase); open the Conjunction node under Noun for particles and iʿrāb rules.
  3. At-tawkīd (التَّوْكِيد) — emphasis / affirmation.
  4. Al-badal (الْبَدَل) — substitute / appositive.

This panel is the overview; Naʿt and Conjunction have their own nodes with full detail.
