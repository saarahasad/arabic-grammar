Conditional Words (Hurūf ash-Sharṭ)

حُرُوفُ الشَّرْط

A conditional sentence has two parts: شَرْط (the condition) and جَوَابُ الشَّرْط (the answer). Conditional clauses are often verbal; the mudāriʿ after these words is usually majzūm (jussive) — often with sukūn (ْ) on the last letter where the indicative would have damma. For لَمْ, لَمَّا, and the general jussive overview, open Jussive particles under Present.

إِنْ — “if” (conditional)

Often introduces a condition; the apodosis may also use a mudāriʿ in jussive depending on structure.

إِنْ تَشْكُرُوا يَرْضَهُ لَكُمْ — If you are grateful, He is pleased with you (39:7)
إِن تَعُودُوا نَعُدْ — If you return, We shall return (8:19)
وَإِن تُطِعْ أَكْثَرَ مَن فِي الْأَرْضِ يُضِلُّوكَ — If you obey most of those on earth, they will lead you astray (6:116)
وَإِن تَعْدِلْ كُلَّ عَدْلٍ لَّا يُؤْخَذْ مِنْهَا — If every compensation were offered… it would not be accepted (6:70)

Past tense after إِذَا, مَنْ, إِنْ

After these words, a past-tense verb may be used while the sense is present or general in context (Quranic style).

إِذَا أَرَادَ شَيْئًا أَن يَقُولَ لَهُ كُن فَيَكُونُ — When He intends a thing, He says to it “Be,” and it is (36:82)
مَن جَاءَ بِالْحَسَنَةِ فَلَهُ عَشْرُ أَمْثَالِهَا — Whoever comes with a good deed will have ten times the like (6:160)
وَإِن كُنتُمْ فِي رَيْبٍ مِّمَّا نَزَّلْنَا عَلَىٰ عَبْدِنَا — And if you are in doubt about what We have sent down upon Our servant… (2:23)

Prefix فَ on جَوَابُ الشَّرْط

Sometimes فَ is prefixed to the verb of the answer clause.

وَإِذَا سَأَلَكَ عِبَادِي عَنِّي فَإِنِّي قَرِيبٌ — And when My servants ask you concerning Me — indeed I am near (2:186)
فَأَمَّا يَتِيمًا فَلَا تَقْهَرْ — So as for the orphan, do not oppress (93:9)

لَوْ and prefix لَ on the answer

The answer to لَوْ may begin with the emphatic لَ attached to the verb.

وَلَوْ شَاءَ رَبُّكَ لَجَعَلَ النَّاسَ أُمَّةً وَاحِدَةً — And if your Lord had willed, He could have made mankind one community (11:118)
وَلَوْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ لَأَعْنَتَكُمْ — And if Allah had willed, He could have put you in difficulty (2:220)

إِلَّا as إِنْ + لَا — “unless / if not”

Besides “except,” إِلَّا can mean unless (if … not). Verbs governed by it in this sense take jazm — both the verb of the condition and the verb of the answer.

إِلَّا تَفْعَلُوهُ تَكُن فِتْنَةٌ فِي الْأَرْضِ — Unless you do it, there would be great corruption in the land (8:73)
إِلَّا تَنصُرُوهُ فَقَدْ نَصَرَهُ اللَّهُ — If you do not help him, Allah has already helped him (9:40)
وَإِلَّا تَغْفِرْ لِي وَتَرْحَمْنِي أَكُن مِّنَ الْخَاسِرِينَ — And unless You forgive me and have mercy on me, I shall be among the losers (11:47)
وَإِلَّا تَصْرِفْ عَنِّي كَيْدَهُنَّ أَصْبُ إِلَيْهِنَّ وَأَكُن مِّنَ الْجَاهِلِينَ — Unless You turn away their plot from me, I shall incline toward them… (12:33)
