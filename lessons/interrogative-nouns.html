Interrogative Nouns

أَسْمَاءُ الِاسْتِفْهَام

These words are called interrogative nouns (أَسْمَاءُ الِاسْتِفْهَام) when they ask questions. They are interrogative nouns only when they stand at the beginning of the sentence (not necessarily the beginning of an āyah).

مَتَى and أَيَّانَ — both “when”

  • مَتَى — used to ask about anything, big or small.
  • أَيَّانَ — used for special, weighty matters.

Qurʾānic examples

أَإِنَّكَ لَأَنْتَ يُوسُفُ — “Are you indeed Yūsuf?” (12:90)
هَلْ عِنْدَكُمْ مِنْ عِلْمٍ — “Do you have any knowledge?” (6:148)
أَمْ حَسِبْتُمْ — “Or do you think…?” (2:214)
مَنْ ذَا الَّذِي يَشْفَعُ عِنْدَهُ — “Who is it that can intercede with Him…?” (2:255)
وَمَا تِلْكَ بِيَمِينِكَ — “What is that in your right hand?” (20:17)
مَاذَا يُنْفِقُونَ — “What should they spend…?” (2:215)
لِمَ تَقُولُونَ مَا لَا تَفْعَلُونَ — “Why do you say what you do not do?” (61:2)
أَنَّى لَكِ هَذَا — “How did you get this?” / “From where (for you) is this?” (3:37)
أَنَّى يَكُونُ لِي وَلَدٌ — “How shall I have a child…?” (3:47)
كَمْ لَبِثْتَ — “How long did you remain…?” (2:259)
لِمَنِ الْمُلْكُ — “For whom is the dominion…?” (40:16)
أَيْنَ شُرَكَاؤُكُمُ — “Where are your partners…?” (6:22)
كَيْفَ تَكْفُرُونَ — “How can you disbelieve…?” (3:101)
أَيُّ شَيْءٍ أَكْبَرُ — “Which thing is greater…?” (6:19)
فَبِأَيِّ آلَاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ — “Then which of your Lord’s favours will you deny?” (55:13)
عَمَّ يَتَسَاءَلُونَ — “About what are they asking?” (78:1)
مِمَّ خُلِقَ — “From what was he created?” (86:5)
مَتَى نَصْرُ اللَّهِ — “When is Allah’s help…?” (2:214)
أَيَّانَ يَوْمُ الدِّينِ — “When is the Day of Recompense?” (51:12)

Exercise: recite the Qurʾān and look for more examples of these words at the start of a sentence.
