Present Tense: Negation & Prohibition

نَفْيُ وَ نَهْيُ الْمُضَارِعِ

With the mudāriʿ, لَا can mean “do not” (prohibition, نَهْيٌ) or “does not / do not” (simple negation, نَفْيٌ). The two uses differ in grammar: prohibition puts the verb in the jussive (مَجْزُوم); negating لَا and مَا usually leave the verb’s endings unchanged.

لَنْ, وَلَمْ, لَا, مَا, and لَيْسَ are also words of negation (along with others covered elsewhere). لَنْ (future negation), لَمْ (past negation on a mudāriʿ shape), and jussive uses appear under Jussive Particles and Iʿrāb (naṣb). لَيْسَ negates the predicate in nominal sentences — “is not / are not” — and is set out below.

Prohibition — لَا + mudāriʿ (نَفْيُ الْفِعْلِ)

Model يَفْعَلُ after prohibitive لَا (jussive shapes — “should not / do not”):

Rule: Prohibitive لَا affects the mudāriʿ like لِـ does for the imperative: the indicative ending moves toward jussive — e.g. final ُ (damma) on the last radical often becomes ْ (sukūn) in the singular; duals end in ا (the indicative ن of يَفْعَلَانِ drops); masculine plurals use وا; feminine plural نَ often stays fixed (مَبْنِيّ).

Simple negation — لَا (no change to the verb)

When لَا means “not” (stating that something does not happen), it does not alter the verb’s endings — the mudāriʿ stays in its normal state (e.g. indicative مَرْفُوع with full endings).

لَا يَرْقُبُونَ فِي مُؤْمِنٍ إِلَّا وَلَا ذِمَّةً — They do not observe toward a believer any kinship or covenant (9:10)
لَا يَسْأَلُونَ النَّاسَ إِلْحَافًا — They do not beg importunately (2:273)
أَلَا تُقَاتِلُونَ قَوْمًا نَّكَثُوا أَيْمَانَهُمْ — Will you not fight a people who broke their oaths… (9:13)
فَلَا تَعْلَمُ نَفْسٌ مَّا أُخْفِيَ لَهُم — And no soul knows what is kept hidden for them (32:17)

Simple negation — مَا (past, present, future)

مَا also negates actions; with present, past, or future it typically does not change the shape of the verb.

وَمَا تُنفِقُونَ إِلَّا ابْتِغَاءَ وَجْهِ اللَّهِ — And you do not spend except seeking Allah’s face (2:272) — مَا + mudāriʿ
وَمَا كَفَرَ سُلَيْمَانُ — And Solomon did not disbelieve… (2:102) — مَا + past

لَيْسَ — “is not / are not” (nominal sentence)

لَيْسَ is a weak / incomplete verb (فِعْلٌ نَاقِصٌ) used to negate the predicate. The conjugation table is in the “past” form, but the meaning is usually present (“is not,” “are not”).

Shape: From the feminine third-person plural column onward, the medial ي of لَيْسَ no longer appears in the written form.
أَلَيْسَ اللَّهُ بِكَافٍ عَبْدَهُ — Is [He] not sufficient for His servant…? (39:36)
وَأَنَّ اللَّهَ لَيْسَ بِظَلَّامٍ لِّلْعَبِيدِ — …and that Allah is not unjust to the servants (3:182)
لَسْتَ عَلَيْهِم بِمُصَيْطِرٍ — You are not over them a controller (88:22)
Contrast: Prohibitive لَا → jussive mudāriʿ; negating لَا and مَا → verb endings usually unchanged.
