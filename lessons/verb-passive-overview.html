Passive verb (overview)

الْفِعْلُ الْمَجْهُول

A verbal sentence (جُمْلَةٌ فِعْلِيَّةٌ) has a verb (فِعْل), a doer (فَاعِل), and (with transitive verbs) an object (مَفْعُول). In the active (فِعْلٌ مَعْرُوف), the doer is stated and the object is manṣūb. In the passive (فِعْلٌ مَجْهُول — literally “unknown” [doer]), the doer is left out; the former object stands as nāʾib al-fāʿil (نَائِبُ الْفَاعِل) and is marfūʿ.

Active vs. passive

شَرِبَ الْوَلَدُ الْمَاءَ—The boy drank the water (active).
شُرِبَ الْمَاءُ—The water was drunk (passive).

Stem vowels (tri-literal model)

From فَعَلَ (active past), passive past is فُعِلَ: damma on the first root letter, kasra on the second.

ضَرَبَ→ضُرِبَ

For the mudāriʿ, passive uses damma on the first root letter and fatha on the second, e.g. يَنْصُرُ → يُنْصَرُ.
