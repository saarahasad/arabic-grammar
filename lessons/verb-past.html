Past Tense

الْمَاضِي

The past tense (الْفِعْلُ الْمَاضِي / الْمَاضِي) describes an action that already happened. Past verbs are mabnī (their endings are fixed by pattern); they do not take rafʿ/naṣb/jarr like nouns.

Pattern فَعَلَ

On the model فَعَلَ, the first and third root letters usually take fatha (َ); the middle letter can be fatha, kasra, or damma — that is how verbs are grouped (e.g. نَصَرَ, سَمِعَ, كَرُمَ).

Middle vowelExamples
Fatha َنَصَرَ، فَتَحَ، كَتَبَ
Kasra ِسَمِعَ، عَلِمَ، فَرِحَ
Damma ُعَظُمَ، كَرُمَ، شَرُفَ

PersonSingularDualPlural

3rd Fem.فَعَلَتْفَعَلَتَافَعَلْنَ
2nd Masc.فَعَلْتَفَعَلْتُمَافَعَلْتُمْ
2nd Fem.فَعَلْتِفَعَلْتُمَافَعَلْتُنَّ
1st (common)فَعَلْتُ—فَعَلْنَا
  • Suffixes attach to the فَعَلَ stem from 3rd person onward; second-person suffixes use تَ, تُمَا, تُمْ, تِ, تُنَّ.
  • First person plural ends in نَا (فَعَلْنَا).
  • Distinguish 3rd fem. plural فَعَلْنَ (short na) from 1st plural فَعَلْنَا (long nā).
  • Every verb carries a built-in subject (e.g. فَعَلَ ≈ “he did”).

Subject after the verb (masculine)

If the subject noun comes after the verb, the verb often stays in the singular masculine form while the noun shows the true number:

فَعَلَ الْمُسْلِمُ — the Muslim did
فَعَلَ الْمُسْلِمَانِ — the two Muslims did
فَعَلَ الْمُسْلِمُونَ — the Muslims did

Subject after the verb (feminine)

If the subject is feminine (singular, dual, or plural) but still comes after the verb, the verb often stays فَعَلَتْ (feminine singular):

فَعَلَتِ الْمُسْلِمَةُ · فَعَلَتِ الْمُسْلِمَتَانِ · فَعَلَتِ الْمُسْلِمَاتُ

Quranic examples — past tense

كَيْفَ فَعَلَ رَبُّكَ — How your Lord did (105:1)
وَقَتَلَ دَاوُدُ جَالُوتَ — And David killed Goliath (2:251)
خَرَجُوا مِنْ دِيَارِهِمْ — They came out of their homes (2:243)
قَالَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا — Those who disbelieved said (19:73)
مَا عَلِمْنَا — We did not know… (12:51) — مَا + past
قَدْ جَاءَ · لَقَدْ خَلَقْنَا — Indeed / certainly with قَدْ / لَقَدْ before the past verb
Before an object pronoun, تُمْ may appear as تُمُو (extra و), e.g. أَدْعَوْتُمُوهُمْ (7:193).
