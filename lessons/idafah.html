Idāfah

الْإِضَافَة

Idāfah (الْإِضَافَة) is the construction where one noun is “annexed” to another to show possession, belonging, or a close link — e.g. “the book of the student,” “Allah’s messenger.” It has two parts: the mudāf (الْمُضَاف) and the mudāf ilayhi (الْمُضَافُ إِلَيْهِ).

الْمُضَافُ إِلَيْهِ (second part)

  • It may take the definite article الْ.
  • It may be indefinite with tanwīn (تَنْوِين) where the construction allows.
  • It is always majrūr (مَجْرُور) — genitive — so it takes the signs of jarr (جَرٌّ).

الْمُضَاف (first part)

  • It cannot have الْ.
  • It cannot have tanwīn.
  • Its ending depends on its role in the sentence: it may be marfūʿ (مَرْفُوع), manṣūb (مَنْصُوب), or majrūr (مَجْرُور).

Signs of jarr on the مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ — original vs. secondary

Endings can be original (أَصْلِيَّة) or secondary (فَرْعِيَّة). For many nouns, jarr uses an original kasrah (كَسْرَة); some word types use secondary endings (e.g. fatḥah or yāʾ) instead of kasrah.

Original jarr — kasrah (أَصْلِيَّة)Secondary endings (فَرْعِيَّة) — fatḥah / yāʾ
الْمُفْرَد — singular nounsالْمُثَنَّى — dual (ـَيْنِ)
جَمْعُ التَّكْسِير — broken pluralجَمْعُ الْمُذَكَّرِ السَّالِم — sound masc. plural (ـِينَ)
جَمْعُ الْمُؤَنَّثِ السَّالِم — sound fem. pluralاسْمٌ غَيْرُ مُنْصَرِفٍ — diptote (fixed pattern; fatḥah in jarr, no tanwīn)

Diptotes (غَيْرُ مُنْصَرِفٍ) do not take tanwīn and use fatḥah where triptotes would use kasrah in jarr — align with your full Iʿrāb reference under Iʿrāb.

Pronoun as مُضَافٌ إِلَيْهِ

When a pronoun (ضَمِير) is suffixed to a noun, the pair is still analyzed as mudāf + mudāf ilayhi: e.g. كِتَابُهُ — “his book” (كِتَاب = mudāf, هُ = mudāf ilayhi).
