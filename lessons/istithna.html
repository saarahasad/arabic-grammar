Exception

الْإِسْتِثْنَاء

An exception (الْإِسْتِثْنَاء) sentence singles out one item from a larger set. The usual particle is إِلَّا (except). The noun after إِلَّا is often manṣūb (مَنْصُوب), but the exact case depends on what comes before إِلَّا.

Three parts

  • Generality (الْمُسْتَثْنَى مِنْهُ) — the whole group you start from; optional in some patterns.
  • Particle of exception (حَرْفُ الِاسْتِثْنَاء) — usually إِلَّا.
  • Excepted item (الْمُسْتَثْنَى) — what is excluded.
أَكَلْتُ الْفَوَاكِهَ إِلَّا عِنَبًا—I ate the fruits except grapes.

الْفَوَاكِهَ = generality; إِلَّا = tool; عِنَبًا = excepted (manṣūb).

Case of the excepted (الْمُسْتَثْنَى)

A — Complete positive statement (the generality group is stated; the clause is not negated): the excepted must be manṣūb.

قَامَ الْقَوْمُ إِلَّا زَيْدًا—The people stood except Zayd.
وَخَرَجَ النَّاسُ إِلَّا عَمْرًا—The people went out except ʿAmr.

B — Complete negative statement (generality stated; clause negated): the excepted may be manṣūb or take the case of badal (same case as the generality noun).

مَا قَامَ الْقَوْمُ إِلَّا زَيْدًا—None of the people stood except Zayd (manṣūb).
مَا قَامَ الْقَوْمُ إِلَّا زَيْدٌ—None of the people stood except Zayd (badal: marfūʿ like الْقَوْمُ).

C — Incomplete negative statement (no explicit generality group; negation with e.g. مَا or لَا): the excepted takes the case it would have without إِلَّا and the negation — marfūʿ, manṣūb, or majrūr according to its role.

مَا جَاءَ إِلَّا زَيْدٌ—No one came except Zayd (subject → marfūʿ).
مَا ضَرَبْتُ إِلَّا زَيْدًا—I struck no one except Zayd (object → manṣūb).
مَا مَرَرْتُ إِلَّا بِزَيْدٍ—I passed by no one except Zayd (after بِ → majrūr).

Incomplete here means the set (الْمُسْتَثْنَى مِنْهُ) is not spelled out as a noun phrase; negative means a particle like مَا or لَا negates the clause.
