Accusative Particles (Hurūf Nāṣibah)

حُرُوفٌ نَاصِبَة

Certain particles (حُرُوفٌ نَاصِبَةٌ) place the following mudāriʿ (الْمُضَارِع) in naṣb (subjunctive): the ending changes compared to the indicative — often fatha on the last letter, or loss of nūn where the indicative would show it. For naṣb on nouns and the general meaning of النَّصْبُ, open Iʿrāb → Naṣb in the mind map.

Not conditional words: Sentences with حُرُوفُ الشَّرْط (conditional words like إِنْ, إِذَا, لَوْ) usually put the mudāriʿ in jazm, not naṣb — see Conditional words or Jussive particles under Present.

Accusative particles (حُرُوفٌ نَاصِبَةٌ)

These particles put the following mudāriʿ into naṣb (subjunctive): visible فَتْحَة on some forms, or loss of final ن in others where the indicative would have it.

Particle(s)Typical meaning
لَنْwill never
حَتَّىٰuntil
أَنْ / لِأَنْ / أَلَّاthat / for that / so that … not
كَيْ / لِكَيْ / لِكَيْلَاso that / so that … not
لِso that (when لَامُ التَّعْلِيلِ)

Signs of naṣb on the mudāriʿ (after these particles)

  • Visible fatha appears on many singular forms (3rd masc./fem., 1st sing.) and 1st plural.
  • For other forms (except some feminine plurals), dropping the nūn that would appear in the indicative can signal naṣb.
  • Presence of nūn usually means rafʿ (indicative); absence of that nūn can mean naṣb or jazm — context and the governing particle decide.

Particle لِ — “so that” (لَامُ التَّعْلِيلِ)

Model verb فَعَلَ in the mudāriʿ after لِ (naṣb shapes):

PersonSingularDualPlural
3rd masculine الْغَائِبُ الْمُذَكَّرُ
—لِيَفْعَلَ (so that he)لِيَفْعَلَالِيَفْعَلُوا
3rd feminine الْغَائِبَةُ الْمُؤَنَّثُ
—لِتَفْعَلَلِتَفْعَلَالِيَفْعَلْنَ
2nd masculine الْحَاضِرُ الْمُذَكَّرُ
—لِتَفْعَلَلِتَفْعَلَالِتَفْعَلُوا
2nd feminine الْحَاضِرَةُ الْمُؤَنَّثُ
—لِتَفْعَلِيلِتَفْعَلَالِتَفْعَلْنَ
1st common الْمُتَكَلِّمُ
—لِأَفْعَلَ—لِنَفْعَلَ
لِيَغْفِرَ لَكَ اللَّهُ — That Allah may forgive you (48:2)
لِيُعَذِّبَ اللَّهُ الْمُنَافِقِينَ — So that Allah may punish the hypocrites (33:73)
لِتَعْلَمُوا عَدَدَ السِّنِينَ — That you might know the number of years (10:5)
Two kinds of لِ: لَامُ الْأَمْرِ (“let / should”) puts the verb in jazm (sukūn), e.g. وَلْيَعْمَلْ صَالِحًا. لَامُ التَّعْلِيلِ (“so that”) puts the verb in naṣb (fatha), e.g. وَلِيَعْمَلَ صَالِحًا — and is not changed to sukūn after وَ / فَ the way imperative لِ is.

كَيْ, لِكَيْ, لِكَيْلَا

كَيْ نُسَبِّحَكَ كَثِيرًا — That we may glorify You much (20:33)
كَيْ لَا يَكُونَ دُولَةً بَيْنَ الْأَغْنِيَاءِ مِنْكُمْ — So that it be not taken by turns by the rich among you (59:7)
لِكَيْلَا تَأْسَوْا عَلَىٰ مَا فَاتَكُمْ — So that you may not despair over what has passed you by (57:23)
لِكَيْ لَا يَعْلَمَ بَعْدَ عِلْمٍ شَيْئًا — So that they know nothing after having known (16:70)

أَنْ, لِأَنْ, أَلَّا

قُلْ مَا يَكُونُ لِي أَنْ أُبَدِّلَهُ — Say, “It is not for me to change it…” (10:15)
وَأُمِرْتُ أَنْ أَكُونَ مِنَ الْمُسْلِمِينَ — And I am commanded to be of those who submit (10:72)
أَلَّا تَخَافُوا وَلَا تَحْزَنُوا — So that you neither fear nor grieve (41:30)
أَنْ لَا أَقُولَ عَلَى اللَّهِ إِلَّا الْحَقَّ — That I do not say about Allah except the truth (7:105)

حَتَّىٰ — “until”

حَتَّىٰ يَمِيزَ الْخَبِيثَ مِنَ الطَّيِّبِ — Until He separates evil from good (3:179)
حَتَّىٰ يَلِجَ الْجَمَلُ فِي سَمِّ الْخِيَاطِ — Until the camel passes through the needle’s eye (7:40)
وَمَا يُعَلِّمَانِ مِنْ أَحَدٍ حَتَّىٰ يَقُولَا — They did not teach anyone until they said… (2:102)
حَتَّىٰ تَسْتَأْنِسُوا — Until you ask permission (24:27)

لَنْ — “will never”

Besides naṣb, لَنْ limits the verb to future and strengthens the negation.

قُلْتُمْ لَنْ يَبْعَثَ اللَّهُ مِنْ بَعْدِهِ رَسُولًا — You said Allah will never raise after him a messenger (40:34)
فَلَنْ يَغْفِرَ اللَّهُ لَهُمْ — So Allah will never forgive them (9:80)
لَنْ تَنَالُوا الْبِرَّ حَتَّىٰ تُنْفِقُوا مِمَّا تُحِبُّونَ — You will never attain righteousness until you spend from what you love (3:92)
فَقُلْ لَنْ تَخْرُجُوا مَعِيَ أَبَدًا — Say, “You shall never come out with me ever” (9:83)
