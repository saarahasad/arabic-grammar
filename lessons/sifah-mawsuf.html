Descriptive phrase

الصِّفَةُ وَالْمَوْصُوفُ

A descriptive phrase is a noun followed by an adjective that describes it. In Arabic grammar the noun is al-mawṣūf (الْمَوْصُوف, “that which is being described”) and the adjective is aṣ-ṣifah (الصِّفَة, “that which describes”). The same idea appears under Naʿt (النَّعْت) as tābiʿ agreement.

In a descriptive phrase the adjective must match the noun in the categories below.

A — Gender

If the noun is masculine or feminine, the adjective agrees.

أَجْرٌ عَظِيمٌ — A great reward — both masculine (e.g. tanwīn ـٌ).
الْمَوْعِظَةُ الْحَسَنَةُ — The good advice — both feminine (ة) with الْ.

B — Number

If the noun is singular, dual, or plural, the adjective agrees.

قُرْآنٌ عَرَبِيٌّ — An Arabic Qurʾān — singular.
رَجُلَانِ صَالِحَانِ — Two righteous men — dual ـَانِ.
آيَاتٌ بَيِّنَاتٌ — Clear signs — sound feminine plural ـَاتٌ.

C — Definiteness

If the noun is definite or indefinite, the adjective matches (both definite or both indefinite).

الْمَسْجِدُ الْحَرَامُ — The sacred Mosque (proper) — both with الْ.
كِتَابٌ مُبِينٌ — An open / manifest book (common) — both indefinite with tanwīn.

D — Iʿrāb (case)

The adjective takes the same case ending as the noun (rafʿ, naṣb, jarr, etc.).

مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ — From the cursed Satan — both majrūr.
عَذَابًا أَلِيمًا — A painful punishment — both manṣūb.
الْعَمَلُ الصَّالِحُ — The righteous deed — both marfūʿ.

Qurʾānic examples

وَإِنَّكَ لَعَلَىٰ خُلُقٍ عَظِيمٍ — And you are surely upon an exalted character. (68:4)
قَالَتْ عَجُوزٌ عَقِيمٌ — She said, “A barren old woman.” (51:29)
بَلْدَةٌ طَيِّبَةٌ وَرَبٌّ غَفُورٌ — A fair land and an oft-forgiving Lord. (34:15)
إِنِّي أُلْقِيَ إِلَيَّ كِتَابٌ كَرِيمٌ — Here is delivered to me a noble letter. (27:29)
وَجَعَلْتُ لَهُ مَالًا مَمْدُودًا — To whom I granted wealth outspread. (74:12)
مِنَ الْمُعْصِرَاتِ مَاءً ثَجَّاجًا — From the clouds water poured forth. (78:14)
لِتَأْكُلُوا مِنْهُ لَحْمًا طَرِيًّا — That you may eat thereof flesh fresh. (16:14)
وَظِلٍّ مَمْدُودٍ — And shade extended. (56:30)
فِي كِتَابٍ مَكْنُونٍ — In a book well guarded. (56:78)
إِلَىٰ مِيقَاتِ يَوْمٍ مَعْلُومٍ — To an appointed time on a day well known. (56:50)
فِي لَوْحٍ مَحْفُوظٍ — In a preserved tablet. (85:22)
ذَلِكَ الْفَوْزُ الْعَظِيمُ — That is the great success. (5:119)
وَالْيَوْمِ الْمَوْعُودِ — By the promised Day. (85:2)
فَأَرَاهُ الْآيَةَ الْكُبْرَىٰ — Then he showed him the greatest sign. (79:20)
يَا أَيَّتُهَا النَّفْسُ الْمُطْمَئِنَّةُ — O reassured soul. (89:27)
فَإِذَا جَاءَتِ الطَّامَّةُ الْكُبْرَىٰ — So when the great overwhelming event comes. (79:34)
مِنَ الشَّجَرِ الْأَخْضَرِ — Out of the green tree. (36:80)
النَّجْمُ الثَّاقِبُ — The piercing star. (86:3)
