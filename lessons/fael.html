Faʿil — Subject / Active participle

الْفَاعِل / اسْمُ الْفَاعِل

Faʿil (الْفَاعِل) is the subject or doer of the verb. The related noun pattern ism al-fāʿil (اسْمُ الْفَاعِل) names “the one who does” the action — the active participle.

Pattern from فَعَلَ

Insert alif after the first root letter; put kasra on the middle letter; the last letter takes tanwīn damma (ٌ) in the indefinite singular.

قَتَلَ→قَاتِلٌ qatilun — killer / one who kills
فَتَحَ→فَاتِحٌ fātiḥun — opener
خَرَجَ→خَارِجٌ khārijun — one who goes out
قَعَدَ→قَاعِدٌ qāʿidun — one who sits

Declension (on the pattern فَاعِل)

SingularDualPlural
Masc.فَاعِلٌفَاعِلَانِفَاعِلُونَ
Fem.فَاعِلَةٌفَاعِلَتَانِفَاعِلَاتٌ
قُلْ يَا أَيُّهَا الْكَافِرُونَ — Say, “O you who disbelieve!” (109:1) — الْكَافِرُونَ is a masculine plural active participle.
