Demonstrative pronouns

أَسْمَاءُ الْإِشَارَة

Asmāʾ al-ishārah (أَسْمَاءُ الْإِشَارَة) are nouns that point to something near (قَرِيب) or far (بَعِيد). They behave as definite (مَعْرِفَة), like proper names.

Most “near” forms begin with هـ; most “far” forms end in كَ.

Near (لِلْقَرِيب)

NumberMasculine مُذَكَّرFeminine مُؤَنَّثMeaning
Singular مُفْرَدهَذَاهَذِهِthis
Dual مُثَنًّىهَذَانِ (rafʿ) / هَذَيْنِ (naṣb / jarr)هَاتَانِ (rafʿ) / هَاتَيْنِ (naṣb / jarr)these two
Plural جَمْعهَؤُلَاءِ (same for masc. & fem.)these (all)

Dual forms in ـانِ are marfūʿ (alif sign of rafʿ); forms in ـيْنِ are manṣūb or majrūr.

Far (لِلْبَعِيد)

NumberMasculineFeminineMeaning
Singularذَلِكَ / ذَالِكَتِلْكَthat
Dualذَانِكَ (rafʿ) / ذَيْنِكَ (naṣb / jarr)تَانِكَ (rafʿ) / تَيْنِكَ (naṣb / jarr)those two
Pluralأُولَئِكَ (same for masc. & fem.)those (all)

In speech and Quranic copy you may see variants like ذَلِكُمَا, ذَلِكُمُ — they are still the same “that” word with different pronoun attachments.

Quranic examples — near

هَذَا كِتَابٌ أَنْزَلْنَاهُ — This is a Book We have revealed (6:155)
قَالُوا إِنَّ هَذَانِ لَسَاحِرَانِ — They said, “These two are [surely] two magicians” (20:63) — dual rafʿ (هَذَانِ)
هَؤُلَاءِ أَهْدَىٰ — These are more guided (4:51)
قُلْ هَذِهِ سَبِيلِي — Say, “This is my path” (12:108)
إِحْدَى ابْنَتَيَّ هَاتَيْنِ — One of these two daughters of mine (28:27) — fem. dual in jarr (هَاتَيْنِ)
هَذِهِ نَاقَةُ اللَّهِ لَكُمْ آيَةً — This she-camel of Allah is a sign for you… (11:64)

Quranic examples — far

ذَلِكَ مَا كُنَّا نَبْغِ — That is what we were seeking (18:64)
ذَلِكُمَا مِمَّا عَلَّمَنِي رَبِّي — That (dual) is of what my Lord has taught me (12:37)
ذَلِكُمُ اللَّهُ رَبُّكُمْ — That is Allah, your Lord (10:3)
فَذَانِكَ بُرْهَانَانِ مِن رَّبِّكَ — So those two are two proofs from your Lord (28:32)
أُولَئِكَ عَلَىٰ هُدًى مِن رَّبِّهِمْ — Those are upon guidance from their Lord (2:5)

Special uses

ذَلِكَ and تِلْكَ (“far”) are sometimes used for something physically near to show greatness, dignity, or importance of what is pointed to.

ذَلِكَ الْكِتَابُ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ — That is the Book in which there is no doubt (2:1)
تِلْكَ آيَاتُ اللَّهِ — Those are the signs of Allah (2:252)
تِلْكَ الرُّسُلُ — Those are the messengers (2:253)

With some plural nouns (including many non-human / broken plurals), Arabic may use the feminine singular demonstrative تِلْكَ for agreement — see the last two examples.
