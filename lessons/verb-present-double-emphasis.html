Double Emphasis (Lām al-tawkīd & heavy nūn)

لَامُ التَّوْكِيدِ وَ النُّونُ الثَّقِيلَةُ

Double emphasis (التَّوْكِيدُ الْمُضَاعَفُ) is placed on the mudāriʿ by prefixing لَ (lām with fatḥah) and suffixing a heavy nūn — نّ with shaddah (and the appropriate vowels). The sense is strong future certainty: “will indeed / shall certainly.”

Vowels before the heavy نّ

  • Fatḥah (بَ) on the letter before نّ in many singulars and in 1st-person forms (e.g. 3rd masc./fem. singular, 2nd masc. singular, 1st sing./pl.).
  • Ḍammah (بُ) before نّ in 2nd and 3rd masculine plural.
  • Kasrah (بِ) before نّ in 2nd feminine singular.
  • Dual forms end in ـانِّ (alif + heavy nūn with kasrah).
  • 2nd/3rd feminine plural often end in ـنَانِّ (nūn + alif + heavy nūn with kasrah).

Quranic examples — mudāriʿ + لَ + heavy نّ

لَأُكَفِّرَنَّ عَنْهُمْ سَيِّئَاتِهِمْ — I shall blot out from them their evil deeds (3:196)
وَلَأُدْخِلَنَّهُمْ جَنَّاتٍ — I shall certainly admit them to gardens (3:196)
فَلَيَعْلَمَنَّ اللَّهُ الَّذِينَ صَدَقُوا — Allah will certainly know those who are true (29:3)
وَلَيَحْمِلُنَّ أَثْقَالَهُمْ — They will certainly bear their burdens (29:13)
وَلَيُسْأَلُنَّ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ — They will be questioned on the Day of Resurrection (29:13)
وَلَنَصْبِرَنَّ عَلَىٰ مَا آذَيْتُمُونَا — We shall certainly bear with patience what you hurt us with (14:12)
لَيَقُولُنَّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا — Those who disbelieved will surely say… (11:7)
لَيَقُولُنَّ إِنَّا كُنَّا مَعَكُمْ — They are sure to say, “We were with you” (29:10)

لَ with nouns, pronouns, and prepositions — إِنَّ + لَ

The heavy نّ attaches only to the mudāriʿ. The emphatic لَ (with fatḥah) can also precede a noun, pronoun, or preposition. In nominal sentences this often appears with إِنَّ, giving double emphasis to the following word.

إِنَّ الْإِنْسَانَ لَفِي خُسْرٍ — Verily, man is in loss (103:2)
إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَغَنِيٌّ عَنِ الْعَالَمِينَ — Allah is free of need from the worlds (29:6)
إِنَّ فِي ذَٰلِكَ لَآيَاتٍ لِقَوْمٍ يُؤْمِنُونَ — Indeed in that are signs for people who believe (30:37)
Heavy nūn vs. emphatic lām: Only the mudāriʿ takes نّ; other words can still take لَ for emphasis, often in إِنَّ sentences.
