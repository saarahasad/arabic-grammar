Verbal Nouns

الْمَصْدَر

Lesson 26. Nouns fall into two broad kinds: primitive (not built from another word) and derived (often from a verb). The usual noun derived from a verb is the verbal noun (مَصْدَر, al-maṣdar) — it names the action or idea of the verb as if it were a thing.

A) Primitive (simple) nouns

Examples of nouns that are not derived from a verb in the usual sense:

Horseفَرَسٌfarasun
Dogكَلْبٌkalbun

B) Verbal nouns (مَصْدَر) from the verb

The verbal noun expresses the verbal meaning in noun form, e.g.:

فَتَحَ (he opened)→فَتْحٌ opening
سَمِعَ (he heard)→سَمْعٌ hearing

Examples in context (Qurʾān)

هَذَا خَلْقُ اللَّهِ — “This is Allah’s creation.” (31:11) — verbal noun: خَلْقُ.
إِنَّ قَتْلَهُمْ كَانَ خِطْأً كَبِيرًا — “Verily, their killing is a great sin.” (17:31) — verbal noun with suffix: قَتْلَهُمْ.

More Qurʾānic examples (verbal nouns underlined in sense)

تُوبُوا إِلَى اللَّهِ تَوْبَةً نَّصُوحًا — “Turn to Allah in sincere repentance.” (66:8) — تَوْبَةً.
إِنَّ سَعْيَكُمْ لَشَتَّىٰ — “Indeed your striving is diverse.” (92:4) — سَعْيَكُمْ.
إِنَّا فَتَحْنَا لَكَ فَتْحًا مُّبِينًا — “Indeed We have granted you a clear victory.” (48:1) — فَتْحًا.
وَصَدٌّ عَن سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ وَكُفْرٌ بِهِ — “…and turning away from Allah’s way and disbelieving in Him…” (2:217) — verbal nouns: صَدٌّ, كُفْرٌ.
الَّذِينَ يَذْكُرُونَ اللَّهَ قِيَامًا وَقُعُودًا — “Those who remember Allah standing and sitting…” (3:191) — قِيَامًا, قُعُودًا.
وَذَٰلِكُمْ ظَنُّكُمُ الَّذِي ظَنَنتُم — “That is your assumption which you assumed.” (41:22) — ظَنٌّ.
