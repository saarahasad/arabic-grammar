Nominal Sentence

الْجُمْلَة الْإِسْمِيَّة

A nominal sentence (الْجُمْلَةُ الْإِسْمِيَّةُ) is a sentence that begins with a noun (اِسْم) — not only “thing” words; pronouns, demonstratives, and other words that behave as nouns can open it. Sentences in Arabic are broadly either nominal (جُمْلَةٌ إِسْمِيَّةٌ) or verbal (جُمْلَةٌ فِعْلِيَّةٌ).

Its two core parts are usually called الْمُبْتَدَأ (subject / “that which is begun”) and الْخَبَر (predicate / “the news” about the subject).

الْمُبْتَدَأ — the subject

  • It is a single noun (not a verb فِعْل or particle حَرْف as the subject slot) and is marfūʿ (مَرْفُوع — nominative).
  • It is usually definite: e.g. pronouns, proper names, nouns with الْ, and similar.
  • It normally stands at the beginning of the nominal sentence (before the predicate).

Sample sentences — subject + predicate:

الطَّالِبُ حَاضِرٌ—The student is present.
الْعِلْمُ نَافِعٌ—Knowledge is beneficial.
الشَّمْسُ طَالِعَةٌ—The sun is rising.
فَاطِمَةُ مُجْتَهِدَةٌ—Fāṭimah is diligent.
الْجَارُ مِنْ دِمَشْقَ كَرِيمٌ—The neighbour from Damascus is generous.

الْمُبْتَدَأ can be mabnī (مَبْنِيّ — indeclinable), e.g. pronouns and some demonstratives: هَؤُلَاءِ مُسْلِمُونَ — These are Muslims; هُوَ مَرِيضٌ — He is sick; ذَلِكَ حَارِسٌ — That is a guard.

الْخَبَر — the predicate

The خَبَر is what is “said” about the مُبْتَدَأ. It usually follows the subject and is often indefinite (when it is a single noun). The predicate agrees with the subject in gender and number.

Two broad kinds of predicate:

  1. Mufrad (مُفْرَد) — a single noun (one اِسْم). Here مُفْرَد means “one word-noun,” not “singular as opposed to plural.”
  2. Other than a single noun (غَيْرُ مُفْرَدٍ) — a phrase or a full sentence acting as predicate.

For a single-noun predicate, the usual rafʿ (رَفْع) signs apply. Phrases and embedded sentences follow their own rules. The خَبَر may also be mabnī (مَبْنِيّ) where appropriate.

Agreement — predicate highlighted:

الطَّالِبُ حَاضِرٌ—The (one male) student is present.
الطُّلَّابُ حَاضِرُونَ—The (male) students are present.
الطَّالِبَةُ حَاضِرَةٌ—The (one female) student is present.
الطَّالِبَانِ حَاضِرَانِ—The two students are present.
الطَّالِبَاتُ حَاضِرَاتٌ—The (female) students are present.

Types of خَبَر

A nominal sentence is مُبْتَدَأ + خَبَر. The predicate may be:

  • Mufrad — one noun, usually marfūʿ or mabnī, e.g. مُحَمَّدٌ أَمِينٌ — Muḥammad is trustworthy.
  • Shibh jumlah (شِبْهُ جُمْلَةٍ) — “phrase-like” predicate, often two words:
    • الْجَارُّ وَالْمَجْرُورُ — preposition + genitive, e.g. مُحَمَّدٌ فِي الْمَدِينَةِ — Muḥammad is in the city.
    • الظَّرْفُ — adverb of time/place, e.g. هِيَ تَحْتَ الْمَكْتَبِ — She is under the desk.
  • Jumlah (جُمْلَةٌ) — a full sentence as predicate:
    • Nominal — e.g. مُحَمَّدٌ أَبُوهُ صَالِحٌ — Muḥammad — his father is righteous.
    • Verbal — e.g. مُحَمَّدٌ قَرَأَ الْقُرْآنَ — Muḥammad read the Qurʾān.

Qurʾānic samples

اللَّهُ نُورُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ — Allah is the Light of the heavens and the earth (24:35)
هَذَا كِتَابٌ أَنْزَلْنَاهُ — This is a Book We have revealed (6:155)
