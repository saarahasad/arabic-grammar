Mabnī & muʿrab — noun endings

الْمَبْنِي وَالْمُعْرَبُ · الْأَسْمَاءُ الْمَبْنِيَّةُ وَالْمُعْرَبَةُ

Nouns in Arabic are either mabnī (مَبْنِيّ) — unchangeable in form — or muʿrab (مُعْرَب) — changeable to show grammatical role (rafʿ, naṣb, jarr).

A) مَبْنِيّ — unchangeable nouns

These words are not affected by what comes before them or by their job in the sentence. They keep the same written form whether they act as subject, object, or come after a preposition.

Examples (single words)

Heهُوَ(huwa)
Theyهُمْ(hum)
That (masc.)ذَلِكَ(dhālika)
Thoseأُولَئِكَ(ulā’ika)
Who / which (masc. sg.)الَّذِي(alladhī)
Who / which (fem. sg.)الَّتِي(allatī)
Mosesمُوسَى(Mūsā)
Jesusعِيسَى(ʿĪsā)

Examples in context (Qurʾān)

And in Moses when We sent himوَفِي مُوسَىٰ إِذْ أَرْسَلْنَهُ
Indeed they sayإِنَّ هَٰؤُلَاءِ لَيَقُولُونَ
Indeed in this is a messageإِنَّ فِي هَذَا لَبَلَاغًا

Many more words in the Qurʾān stay the same in every situation; they belong to the mabnī group.

B) مُعْرَب — changeable nouns

Changeable nouns are of two kinds.

1. مُنْصَرِف — fully changeable (triptote)

These nouns change in all three main situations. In the Qurʾān you will see them ending with short vowels َ / ُ / ِ or with tanwīn ً / ٌ / ٍ.

Example: the name Nūḥ (نُوح)

Rafʿ (nom.) — tanwīn ḍammahإِذْ قَالَ لَهُمْ أَخُوهُمْ نُوحٌ26:106
Naṣb (acc.) — tanwīn fatḥahوَلَقَدْ أَرْسَلْنَا نُوحًا29:14
Jarr (gen.) — tanwīn kasrahقَوْمِ نُوحٍ(people of Noah)

Many other words in the Qurʾān that show a full three-way change belong to the munṣarif type of muʿrab.

2. غَيْرُ مُنْصَرِف — partly changeable (diptote / مَمْنُوعٌ مِنَ الصَّرْف)

These differ from fully changeable nouns in two ways:

  • They do not end with tanwīn (ً / ٌ / ٍ).
  • They only end with ُ or َ — never with ِ on the last letter. When the grammar would call for kasrah (e.g. jarr), they use fatḥah instead.

Examples with Firʿawn (فِرْعَوْن)

The companions of Pharaohأَصْحَابُ فِرْعَوْنَ
They denied Pharaohكَذَّبُوا فِرْعَوْنَ
Pharaoh saidقَالَ فِرْعَوْنُ

Other words of this type (among many in the Qurʾān):

Scholarsعُلَمَاءُ
Maryمَرْيَمُ
Abrahamإِبْرَاهِيمُ
Josephيُوسُفُ
Davidدَاوُودُ
Solomonسُلَيْمَانُ

Words that never take tanwīn or kasrah on the last letter belong to the ghayr munṣarif group of muʿrab — see also the mind map node Al-mamnuʿ min al-ṣarf.

C) مَبْنِيّ in analysis — “change” vs fixed form

In full إِعْرَاب, you will often write mabnī (مَبْنِيّ) for nouns in two different situations. Keeping them separate stops confusion.

1. مَبْنِيّ عَلَى … — fixed vowel on the last letter

Many nouns and pronouns never change shape for rafʿ / naṣb / jarr. You state مَبْنِيّ عَلَى الْفَتْحِ / الْكَسْرِ / الضَّمِّ / السُّكُونِ (as your teacher prescribes). Examples: detached pronouns (هُوَ، هِيَ، أَنْتَ), many demonstratives and relatives (هَذَا، ذَلِكَ، الَّذِي), and certain proper names (مُوسَىٰ، عِيسَىٰ). Their “case” is not shown by a different ending on the word itself.

2. مَبْنِيّ فِي مَحَلِّ … — fixed form, role in the sentence

Later lessons describe words that stay mabnī in form but fill a slot that would normally be rafʿ, naṣb, or jarr (e.g. إِنَّنِي broken into parts, or a pronoun inside a verb). Teachers write مَبْنِيّ … فِي مَحَلِّ رَفْعٍ / نَصْبٍ / جَرٍّ — mabnī, but in the place of … That is not the same as مُعْرَبٌ مَرْفُوعٌ on the last letter; the analysis names the role without a triptote ending.

If your exam only asks for مَبْنِيّ vs مُعْرَب, use section A–B above. Add فِي مَحَلِّ … only when your syllabus has taught إِعْرَاب الْمَحَلِّ.

Note: The grammatical ending (إِعْرَاب) of a noun is never sukūn (ْ).
