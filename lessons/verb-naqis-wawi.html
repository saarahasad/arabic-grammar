Defective Verb (Last Letter Wāw)

الْفِعْلُ النَّاقِصُ الْوَاوِيُ

A defective (نَاقِص) verb has wāw (و) as its last root letter. In the past tense the third radical often appears as a long alif (ا) in the third-person masculine singular — e.g. دَعَا “to call / invite” from root د-ع-و. Dual and plural past forms restore و before suffixes.

From third-person masculine dual onward, the long ا of دَعَا is written as the original و before the suffix (دَعَوَا, دَعَوْا, …). There is no first-person dual; “we” uses the plural.

Imperative (الْأَمْرُ)

SingularDualPlural
Masc.اُدْعُاُدْعُوَااُدْعُوا
Fem.اُدْعِياُدْعُوَااُدْعُونَ
In the imperative, و is dropped in the masculine singular (اُدْعُ) as a sign of سُكُون. In other forms, the ن of the sound pattern may drop (showing sukūn), except in the feminine plural.

Summary rules (textbook)

  1. In the past, the alif of the singular base becomes the original و from third-person masculine dual onward.
  2. In the mudāriʿ, third-person masculine and feminine plurals match; second-person masculine and feminine plurals match.
  3. In the imperative, masculine singular drops final و; other forms (except feminine plural) often drop written ن where the pattern has sukūn.

Quranic examples — root دَعَا

وَمَنْ أَحْسَنُ قَوْلًا مِّمَّن دَعَا إِلَى اللَّهِ — Who is better in speech than one who calls to Allah? (41:33)
دَعَوَا اللَّهَ رَبَّهُمَا لَئِنْ آتَيْتَنَا صَالِحًا — They both called upon Allah their Lord… (7:189)
وَإِذَا مَسَّ النَّاسَ ضُرٌّ دَعَوْا رَبَّهُم — When harm touches people, they cry to their Lord. (30:33)
قَالَ رَبِّ إِنِّي دَعَوْتُ قَوْمِي — He said, “My Lord, I called my people…” (71:5)
وَبَلَوْنَاهُم بِالْحَسَنَاتِ وَالسَّيِّئَاتِ — We tested them with good and evil. (7:168)
وَاللَّهُ يَدْعُو إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ وَالْمَغْفِرَةِ — Allah calls to Paradise and forgiveness. (2:221)

More Quranic samples (last-letter و verbs)

أُولَٰئِكَ يَدْعُونَ إِلَى النَّارِ — They invite to the Fire. (2:221)

وَإِذَا دُعُوا إِلَى اللَّهِ وَرَسُولِهِ — When they are called to Allah and His Messenger… (24:48)
إِذَا دُعِيتُمْ فَادْخُلُوا — When you are invited, enter. (33:52)
أَدْعُ إِلَىٰ سَبِيلِ رَبِّكَ بِالْحِكْمَةِ — Invite to the way of your Lord with wisdom. (16:125)
ادْعُوهُمْ لِآبَائِهِمْ — Call them by (the names of) their fathers. (33:5)
وَلَا تَقْفُ مَا لَيْسَ لَكَ بِهِ عِلْمٌ — Pursue not that of which you have no knowledge. (17:36)
وَلَنَبْلُوَنَّكُم بِشَيْءٍ مِّنَ الْخَوْفِ — We shall try you with something of fear. (2:155)
