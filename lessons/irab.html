I'rāb

الْإِعْرَاب

I'rāb is the system of grammatical states in Arabic: how the end of a word changes to show its role in the sentence. There are four main types.

The four types — sign and name of the word

Type of I'rābOriginal signWord in this state
الرَّفْعُالضَّمَّةُ
Ex: بَيْتٌ		مَرْفُوعٌ
النَّصْبُالْفَتْحَةُ
Ex: بَيْتًا		مَنْصُوبٌ
الْجَرُّالْكَسْرَةُ
Ex: بَيْتٍ		مَجْرُورٌ
الْجَزْمُالسُّكُونُ
Ex: لَمْ أَذْهَبْ		مَجْزُومٌ

Where each type appears

TypeFound onExample
الرَّفْعُNouns (الْأَسْمَاءُ) and the present/future verb (الْفِعْلُ الْمُضَارِعُ)الطَّالِبُ يَدْرُسُ — The student is studying
النَّصْبُNouns and the present/future verbلَنْ أَفْعَلَ إِلَّا الْخَيْرَ — I shall do nothing but good
الْجَرُّNouns only (الْأَسْمَاءُ)فِي الْبَيْتِ — in the house
الْجَزْمُPresent/future verb only (الْفِعْلُ الْمُضَارِعُ)لَمْ يَذْهَبْ — He did not go
Note: All حُرُوف (particles) and the past (الْمَاضِي) and imperative (الْأَمْرُ) verb forms are mabnī (مَبْنِيّ) — their endings are fixed and do not change for case.
