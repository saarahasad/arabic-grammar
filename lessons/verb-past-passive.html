Past Passive

الْمَاضِي الْمَجْهُول

In the passive (فِعْلٌ مَجْهُول), the doer is unknown or left unstressed; the sentence stresses what happened to someone or something. The active (فِعْلٌ مَعْرُوف) names the doer clearly.

Vowel pattern (tri-literal)

From the model فَعَلَ (active), the basic passive past is فُعِلَ: damma on the first root letter, kasra on the second, fatha on the third.

Active pastPassive past
Templateفَعَلَفُعِلَ
Transitive only: Only transitive verbs (فِعْلٌ مُتَعَدٍّ) — those that take an object — form a passive. Intransitive verbs (لَازِم), e.g. كَذَبَ, مَاتَ, have no passive of this kind.

نَائِبُ الْفَاعِل

When the passive verb has no expressed subject, the object of the action (what would follow an active verb) often stands in the place of the subject grammatically — the “deputy subject” (نَائِبُ الْفَاعِل), typically with rafʿ marked by damma on the definite noun.

Quranic examples — passive past

كُتِبَ عَلَيْكُمُ الصِّيَامُ — Prescribed for you is fasting… (2:183)
وَحُشِرَ لِسُلَيْمَانَ جُنُودُهُ — And gathered for Solomon were his hosts… (27:17)
وَجُمِعَ الشَّمْسُ وَالْقَمَرُ — And the sun and moon are brought together… (75:9)
أَمْ خُلِقُوا مِنْ غَيْرِ شَيْءٍ — Or were they created by nothing? (52:35)
وَإِذَا الصُّحُفُ نُشِرَتْ — And when the pages are spread… (81:10)
وَلَئِن قُتِلْتُمْ أَوْ قَتَلْتُمْ — And if you are killed or kill… (3:157)
خُلِقَ الْإِنْسَانُ ضَعِيفًا — Man was created weak… (4:28)
