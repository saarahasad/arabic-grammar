Hollow Verb (Middle Wāw)

الْفِعْلُ الْأَجْوَفُ الْوَاوِيُ

A hollow verb (الْفِعْلُ الْأَجْوَفُ) has wāw (و) or yāʾ (ي) as its middle root letter. In the past tense it often appears as a long alif (ا) without full إِعْرَاب vowel marks — a spelling that reflects easier pronunciation. E.g. قَالَ comes from the root قَوَلَ; بَاعَ from بَيَعَ.

From the third-person feminine plural row downward in the past, the middle ا is dropped and the vowel on the first root letter often shifts from فَتْحَة to ضَمَّة (قُلْنَ, قُلْتَ, …).

In the mudāriʿ, the middle ا of the past is usually realized as و (يَقُولُ); in feminine plurals the و may also drop (يَقُلْنَ, تَقُلْنَ).

Imperative (الْأَمْرُ)

SingularDualPlural
Masc.قُلْقُولَاقُولُوا
Fem.قُولِيقُولَاقُلْنَ
In the imperative, و is dropped in the masculine singular (قُلْ) and in the feminine plural (قُلْنَ).

Summary rules (textbook)

  1. In the past, from third-person feminine plural through the rest of the table, the alif drops and the vowel on the first root letter often becomes damma.
  2. In the mudāriʿ, the alif is replaced by wāw, except in feminine plurals where wāw may also drop.
  3. In the imperative, wāw drops in masculine singular and feminine plural only.

Quranic examples — root قَالَ

إِذْ قَالَ اللَّهُ يَا عِيسَىٰ — And when Allah said, “O ʿĪsā…” (3:55)
قَالُوا رَبُّنَا اللَّهُ — They said, “Our Lord is Allah.” (41:30)
قَالَتْ رَبِّ أَنَّىٰ يَكُونُ لِي وَلَدٌ — She said, “My Lord! How can I have a son?” (3:47)
قُلْنَ حَاشَ لِلَّهِ مَا عَلِمْنَا عَلَيْهِ مِن سُوءٍ — They said, “By Allah, we knew no evil on his part.” (12:51)
أَأَنتَ قُلْتَ لِلنَّاسِ — Did you say to the people…? (5:116)
وَإِذْ قُلْتُمْ يَا مُوسَىٰ — And when you said, “O Mūsā…” (2:61)
وَإِذْ قُلْنَا لِلْمَلَائِكَةِ اسْجُدُوا لِآدَمَ — And when We said to the angels, “Bow down to Adam…” (2:34)

More Quranic samples (hollow و verbs)

ثُمَّ لَا يَمُوتُ فِيهَا وَلَا يَحْيَىٰ — Then he will neither die nor live therein. (87:13)
فَآخَرَانِ يَقُومَانِ مَقَامَهُمَا — Two others stand forth in their places… (5:107)
وَيَقُولُونَ مَتَىٰ هَٰذَا الْوَعْدُ — They say, “When will this promise come true?” (36:48)
إِن تَعُودُوا نَعُدْ — If you return (to the attack), so shall we. (8:19)
وَتُبْ عَلَيْنَا — And turn unto us (in mercy)… (2:128)
فَقُولَا لَهُ قَوْلًا لَّيِّنًا — Speak to him mildly. (20:44)
أَن يَقُولَ لَهُ كُن فَيَكُونُ — That He says to it “Be,” and it is. (36:82)
لَا تَكُونُوا كَالَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا — Do not be like those who disbelieved. (3:156)
مَا يُقَالُ لَكَ إِلَّا مَا قَدْ قِيلَ لِلرُّسُلِ — Nothing is said to you except what was said to the messengers before. (41:43)
Shortening: For يَكُونُ, the last letters may drop in speech or script (e.g. فَلَا تَكُ فِي مِرْيَةٍ “be not in doubt” — 11:17).
