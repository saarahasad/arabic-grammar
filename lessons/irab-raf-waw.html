Wāw — secondary sign of Raf'

الْوَاوُ

الْوَاوُ is a secondary sign of Raf' for sound masculine plural and for the five nouns when muḍāf.

Sound masculine plural (جَمْعُ الْمُذَكَّرِ السَّالِمِ)

Plural with ـوْنَ; the wāw is part of the visible ending in Raf'.

مُؤْمِنُونَMu'minūna
الْمُدَرِّسُونَAl-mudarrisūna
مُسْلِمُونَMuslimūna

The five nouns (الْأَسْمَاءُ الْخَمْسَةُ)

When muḍāf, Raf' often shows و rather than a plain dammah on the base.

أَبٌ, أَخٌ, حَمُو, فَمٌ, ذُو

أَبُوكَAbūka
أَخُوكَAkhūka
حَمُوكَHamūka
فُوكَFūka (فَمٌ drops mīm when muḍāf)
ذُو عِلْمٍDhū ʿilmin
Note: ذُو and حَمُو are often muḍāf by nature; the others take secondary signs when the muḍāf condition is met.
