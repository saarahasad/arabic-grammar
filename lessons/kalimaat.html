Kalimaat (Words)

كَلِمَات / اَلْكَلَام

Arabic speech (اَلْكَلَام) is made up of three types of words:

اِسْم (ism) — a noun
فِعْل (fi'l) — a verb
حَرْف (harf) — a particle (prepositions, etc.)

Note: There are many different sub-categories for each. Click Noun, Verb, or Particle in the mind map to explore.

1. Noun — الْإِسْم (Al-Ism)

It is the name of an object, place, person, or of an action, e.g.:

A bookكِتَابٌ(kitaabun)
Muhammadمُحَمَّدٌ(muhammadun)
Heهُوَ(huwa)
Standingقِيَامٌ(qiyaamun)

2. Verb — الْفِعْل (Al-Fi'l)

It tells about an act with reference to past, present, or future tense, e.g.:

He wentذَهَبَ(dhahaba)
He does / He will doيَفْعَلُ(yaf'alu)

3. Particle — الْحَرْف (Al-Harf)

It is a word that can be used with a noun or a verb, e.g.:

Inفِي
Whoمَنْ
Ifإِنْ
Whenإِذَا
