Geminate (Double-Lettered) Verb

الْفِعْلُ الْمُضَاعَفُ

A geminate verb (الْفِعْلُ الْمُضَاعَفُ / فِعْلٌ مُضَاعَفٌ) has the second and third root letters the same (e.g. root m–d–d “extend”). In many forms the double letter is written with shaddah (ّ); in some persons the two letters are spelled out separately.

Past tense (الْمَاضِي) — root مَدَّ “to extend / spread”

PersonSingularDualPlural
3rd Masc.مَدَّمَدَّامَدُّوا
3rd Fem.مَدَّتْمَدَّتَامَدَدْنَ
2nd Masc.مَدَدْتَمَدَدْتُمَامَدَدْتُمْ
2nd Fem.مَدَدْتِمَدَدْتُمَامَدَدْتُنَّ
1st (common)مَدَدْتُ—مَدَدْنَا
In the 3rd-person forms above, the geminate is often merged with shaddah; in 2nd and 1st persons the root letters appear separately (مَدَدْتَ, etc.).
وَهُوَ الَّذِي مَدَّ الْأَرْضَ — And it is He who spread out the earth (13:3)

Mudāriʿ (الْمُضَارِع) — same root: jussive after لَمْ, subjunctive after لَنْ, indicative

Columns: after لَمْ (jussive / sukūn), after لَنْ (naṣb), bare indicative (مَرْفُوع).

After لَمْAfter لَنْIndicative

3rd Fem. / 2nd Masc.لَمْ تَمْدُدْلَنْ تَمُدَّتَمُدُّ
2nd Fem.لَمْ تَمْدُدِيلَنْ تَمْدُدِيتَمُدِّينَ
1stلَمْ أَمْدُدْلَنْ أَمُدَّأَمُدُّ

More Quranic samples (geminate roots)

وَاشْدُدْ عَلَىٰ قُلُوبِهِمْ — And harden their hearts… (10:88)
وَاغْضُضْ مِن صَوْتِكَ — And lower your voice… (31:19)
وَلَا تَمُدَّنَّ عَيْنَيْكَ — Strain not your eyes (20:131)
وَظِلٍّ مَّمْدُودٍ — In shade long extended (56:30)
