Verbs with First Letter Wāw

الْفِعْلُ الْمِثَالُ (الْوَاوِيُ)

When the first root letter is و, it often drops in the مُضَارِع and is replaced by the normal mudāriʿ prefix vowels (يَعِدُ from root و-ع-د, past وَعَدَ). These are often called assimilated (مِثَال) verbs in traditional grammar.

Model: past وَعَدَ “to promise” — present يَعِدُ

Quranic samples — initial و verbs

وَوَضَعَ الْمِيزَانَ — And He has set up the balance… (55:7)
إِذَا وَقَعَتِ الْوَاقِعَةُ — When the Event occurs… (56:1)
رَبِّ إِنِّي وَضَعْتُهَا أُنثَىٰ — My Lord, I have delivered a female. (3:36)
وَوَهَبْنَا لَهُ إِسْحَاقَ وَيَعْقُوبَ — And We granted him Ishāq and Yaʿqūb. (21:72)
يَهَبُ لِمَن يَشَاءُ إِنَاثًا — He grants females to whom He wills. (42:49)
وَلَا يَجِدُونَ عَنْهَا مَحِيصًا — They will not find any escape from it. (4:121)
هَٰذِهِ جَهَنَّمُ الَّتِي كُنتُمْ تُوعَدُونَ — This is Hell which you were promised. (36:63)
مَّثَلُ الْجَنَّةِ الَّتِي وُعِدَ الْمُتَّقُونَ — The likeness of the Garden promised to the righteous… (13:35)
