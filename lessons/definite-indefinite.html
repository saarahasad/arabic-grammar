Definite & Indefinite Nouns

نَكِرَة وَ مَعْرِفَة

Nouns in Arabic can either be Indefinite (نَكِرَةٌ) or Definite (مَعْرِفَةٌ).

Indefinite — نَكِرَةٌ

كُلُّ اسْمٍ يَدُلُّ عَلَىٰ غَيْرِ مُعَيَّنٍ

Nouns that indicate something unspecific.

كِتَابٌ

A book

Definite — مَعْرِفَةٌ

كُلُّ اسْمٍ يَدُلُّ عَلَىٰ مُعَيَّنٍ

Nouns that indicate something specific.

الْكِتَابُ

The book

How do you say "the" in Arabic?

You add ال at the beginning of the word.

كِتَابٌ A book → الْكِتَابُ The book

Indefinite marker: تَنْوِين (Tanween)

An indefinite noun is indicated by تَنْوِين — the extra نون (noon) added at the end of a word, which is pronounced yet not written. It is represented by the doubling of the vowel.

بَيْتٌ
Bait-un
بَيْتًا
Bait-an
بَيْتٍ
Bait-in

Definite marker: ال (Al-)

A definite noun is indicated by ال at the beginning.

الْبَيْتُ Al-Baitu — The house
الْكِتَابُ Al-Kitabu — The book
Note: The ال and the تَنْوِين can never coexist. When ال comes, the تَنْوِين goes away.
Exception: Some definite nouns take تَنْوِين, e.g. proper names like مُحَمَّدٌ (Muhammad).
