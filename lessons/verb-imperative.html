The Imperative

فِعْلُ الْأَمْر

The imperative (فِعْلُ الْأَمْر) expresses command, order, or request. This form is used for the second person only (you / you two / you all — masculine or feminine). It is derived from the present tense (الْمُضَارِع) by the following method:

I. Take the verb of the second person in the present tense.

II. Remove the first letter of the present tense — one of the four letters of أَنِيت (أَ، نَ، يَ، تَ).

III. Add a hamza at the beginning (هَمْزَةُ الْوَصْل). The vowel on this hamza is decided by the harakah on the third letter of the word (the last root letter in a tri-literal verb, e.g. ل in فْعَلُ): if it has ḍammah, the hamza takes ḍammah (أُ); otherwise it takes kasrah (أِ) — e.g. اُقْعُدْ, اِفْعَلْ.

IV. Replace ḍammah on the last letter with sukūn, or remove the nūn — i.e. use the jussive (مَجْزُوم) shape of the mudāriʿ: singular often ends in sukūn; in dual and plural the indicative ن drops where it would mark the indicative (feminine plural keeps نَ).

Imperatives are mabnī (fixed ending). For “let him / her / them / me / us” with لِـ, open Lam al-amr in the mind map.

More examples (hamza voweling)

  • Kasrah on hamza when the last root letter would have fatḥah or kasrah in the relevant mudāriʿ shape: اِضْرِبْ, اِسْمَعْ.
  • Ḍammah on hamza when that letter has ḍammah: اُقْعُدْ, اُخْرُجْ.
  • Feminine plural keeps نَ: اِفْعَلْنَ.

هَمْزَةُ الْوَصْل in speech

After a preceding word (e.g. وَ, فَ), this hamza is often not pronounced as a full glottal stop; the phrase flows — e.g. فَافْعَلُوا, وَاذْكُرُوا, فَاعْلَمْ.

Quranic samples

اِفْعَلْ — Do! (masc. sing.)
فَافْعَلُوا — So do (masc. pl.)
وَاذْكُرُوا — And remember (masc. pl.)
وَاذْكُرْنَ — And remember (fem. pl.)
ارْجِعِي — Return! (fem. sing.)
