Conjunction (Al-ʿAṭf)

الْعَطْف

Al-ʿaṭf (الْعَطْف) is the joining of two words or clauses with a conjunction particle (حَرْفُ الْعَطْف). Common examples include وَ “and,” فَ “then / so,” ثُمَّ “then (after a gap),” أَوْ “or,” and بَلْ “rather / on the contrary.” The particle adds a specific meaning to the link between the two parts.

Main conjunction particles

Course notes often give ten conjunction particles; some analyses also treat حَتَّىٰ as a conjunction in certain uses. Exact lists vary slightly by text — the table below follows a common teaching order.

الْمَعْطُوفُ عَلَيْهِ and الْمَعْطُوفُ

  • Al-maʿṭūf ʿalayh (الْمَعْطُوفُ عَلَيْهِ) — the first member, before the conjunction.
  • Al-maʿṭūf (الْمَعْطُوفُ) — the second member, after the conjunction.
سَافَرَ أَحْمَدُ وَخَالِدٌ — Aḥmad and Khālid traveled. Here أَحْمَدُ is al-maʿṭūf ʿalayh; خَالِدٌ is al-maʿṭūf. Both names share the same syntactic role, so both are marfūʿ (مَرْفُوع) as subjects.

Common حُرُوفُ الْعَطْف — meanings and examples

ParticleMeaning & usageExampleGloss
وَ (wāw)And — simple coordination.جَاءَ مُحَمَّدٌ وَخَالِدٌMuḥammad and Khālid came.
فَ (fāʾ)Then / so — immediate sequence, one after another.جَاءَ خَالِدٌ، فَعَلِيٌّ، فَطَارِقٌKhālid came, then ʿAlī, then Ṭāriq.
ثُمَّThen — after a delay or later in time.سَافَرَ زَيْدٌ ثُمَّ خَالِدٌZayd traveled; later Khālid did.
أَوْOr — choice between two options.جِئْنِي بِكِتَابٍ أَوْ دَفْتَرٍBring me a book or a notebook.
أَمْOr — in questions (which of two?).أَدْرَسْتَ الْفِقْهَ أَمِ النَّحْوَ؟Did you study fiqh or naḥw?
إِمَّاEither … or … (often paired with a second إِمَّا).تَزَوَّجْ إِمَّا هِنْدًا وَإِمَّا أُخْتَهَاMarry either Hind or her sister.
بَلْRather / instead — correction or turning to a truer alternative (إِضْرَاب).لَمْ آكُلْ لَحْمًا، بَلْ بَيْضًاI did not eat meat; rather eggs.
لَاNot — excludes the second after affirming the first (“X came, not Y”).جَاءَ زَيْدٌ لَا مُحَمَّدٌZayd came, not Muḥammad.
لَكِنْBut — often after a negative or question; restriction or contrast.مَا أَكَلْتُ عِنَبًا لَكِنْ تُفَّاحًاI did not eat grapes, but apples.
حَتَّىٰUp to / including / even — endpoint or climax in a series.أَكَلْتُ السَّمَكَةَ حَتَّىٰ رَأْسَهَاI ate the fish down to its head.

Some lists count slightly different particles or merge senses of لَا; follow your teacher’s list if it differs.

Rule of iʿrāb — the مَعْطُوف follows the مَعْطُوفٌ عَلَيْهِ

The coordinated noun after the conjunction takes the same case as the noun before it: if the first is marfūʿ, the second is marfūʿ; likewise manṣūb and majrūr.

رَأَيْتُ زَيْدًا وَعَمْرًا — I saw Zayd and ʿAmr — both manṣūb (مَنْصُوب) as objects of رَأَيْتُ.
مَرَرْتُ بِزَيْدٍ وَعَمْرٍ — I passed by Zayd and ʿAmr — both majrūr (مَجْرُور) after the preposition بِ.
