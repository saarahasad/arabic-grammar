Lam al-amr (لِـ)

لَامُ الْأَمْر

To express a command or wish for the first or third person (“let him…”, “let them…”, “let me…”), Arabic prefixes لِـ (lām al-amr) to the mudāriʿ and puts the verb in the jussive (مَجْزُوم) shape: singular — sukūn on the last radical; dual / plural — drop the indicative ن where that applies (feminine plural keeps نَ).

Conjugation (يَفْعَلُ)

PersonSingularDualPlural
3rd masculine
—لِيَفْعَلْ (let him / he should)لِيَفْعَلَالِيَفْعَلُوا
3rd feminine
—لِتَفْعَلْلِتَفْعَلَالِيَفْعَلْنَ
1st (masc. & fem.)
—لِأَفْعَلْ (let me)—لِنَفْعَلْ (let us)

After وَ or فَ

The لَام often loses its kasra and is read with sukūn: وَلْـ (wal-), فَلْـ (fal-).

وَلْيَتَّقِ اللَّهَ رَبَّهُ — And let him fear Allah, his Lord (2:286)
فَلْيُقَاتِلْ فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ — So let him fight in the way of Allah (4:74)
لِيَسْتَأْذِنكُمُ — Let them ask your permission (24:58) — sentence-initial لِـ with kasra
Contrast: The plain 2nd-person imperative (اِفْعَلْ) does not use لِـ; لِـ + mudāriʿ is for “let / should” with 1st and 3rd persons.
