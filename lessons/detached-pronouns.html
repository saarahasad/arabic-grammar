Detached Pronouns

ضَمَائِر مُنْفَصِلَة

Detached pronouns stand alone; they are not attached to another word.

Table of Detached Pronouns

PersonSingularDualPlural
Third Person (الْغَائِب) — Absent
Masculineهُوَ (He, it)هُمَا (They two)هُمْ (They)
Feminineهِيَ (She, it)هُمَا (They two)هُنَّ (They)
Second Person (الْحَاضِر) — Addressed
Masculineأَنْتَ (You)أَنْتُمَا (You two)أَنْتُمْ (You all)
Feminineأَنْتِ (You)أَنْتُمَا (You two)أَنْتُنَّ (You all)
First Person (الْمُتَكَلِّم) — Speaker
Masc. & Fem.أَنَا (I)نَحْنُ (We)

Memorization Rules

Rule 1: All third-person pronouns (masc. & fem.) begin with هـ (Hā).
Rule 2: All second-person pronouns (masc. & fem.) begin with أ ن ت (Alif, Nūn, Tā — "Anta").
Rule 3: All dual pronouns end with مَا (Mā).
Rule 4: Masculine plural (2nd & 3rd person) end with مُ (Mīm with damma).
Rule 5: Feminine plural (2nd & 3rd person) end with نَّ (Nūn with shadda).

Quranic Examples

هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ — He is Allah, the One. (112:1)
إِذْ هُمَا فِي الْغَارِ — When they (two) were in the cave. (9:40)
أَنَا رَبُّكُمُ الْأَعْلَىٰ — I am your most exalted Lord. (79:24)
نَحْنُ نَرْزُقُكُمْ — We provide for you. (6:151)
