Plural

جَمْع

The plural (جَمْع) refers to three or more entities. Arabic plurals fall into two broad types: sound (جَمْعٌ سَالِمٌ) and broken (جَمْعٌ مُكَسَّرٌ, also called جَمْعُ التَّكْسِيرِ).

1. Sound plural (جَمْعٌ سَالِمٌ)

The singular stem stays intact; endings are added after the last letter. For dual, add ـَانِ (rafʿ) or ـَيْنِ (naṣb / jarr). For a sound masculine plural, add ـُوْنَ (rafʿ) or ـِيْنَ (naṣb / jarr).

For feminine nouns ending in ة (tāʾ marbūṭah), the ة is written as ت before dual suffixes; the sound feminine plural uses ـَاتٌ / ـَاتٍ (with the appropriate case vowels).

Example: مُسْلِمٌ — “a Muslim” (masc.)

SingularDualPlural (sound masc.)
Masc. مُسْلِمٌ مُسْلِمَانِ / مُسْلِمَيْنِ مُسْلِمُوْنَ / مُسْلِمِيْنَ
Fem. مُسْلِمَةٌ مُسْلِمَتَانِ / مُسْلِمَتَيْنِ مُسْلِمَاتٌ / مُسْلِمَاتٍ

Qurʾānic examples — sound plurals & related

وَمَنْ يَقْتُلْ مُؤْمِنًا — …And whoever kills a believer… (4:93)
قَدْ أَفْلَحَ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ — Indeed the believers have succeeded. (23:1)
وَلَأَمَةٌ مُؤْمِنَةٌ خَيْرٌ — …And a believing bondwoman is better… (2:221)
جَنَّتَيْنِ ذَوَاتَيْ أُكُلٍ خَمْطٍ — Two gardens bearing bitter fruit… (34:16)
فَتَحْرِيرُ رَقَبَةٍ مُؤْمِنَةٍ — …The freeing of a believing neck (slave)… (4:92)
وَمِنْ ذُرِّيَّتِنَا أُمَّةً مُسْلِمَةً لَكَ — …And from our progeny a community submissive to You… (2:128)
جَنَّتَانِ عَنْ يَمِينٍ — Two gardens on the right… (34:15)
فَالصَّالِحَاتُ قَانِتَاتٌ — So the righteous women are devoutly obedient… (4:34)

2. Broken plural (جَمْعٌ مُكَسَّرٌ)

The dual of a noun that has a broken plural is still formed with ـَانِ / ـَيْنِ on the singular stem (like sound plurals). The plural itself, however, often changes the internal vowels and letters — there is no single rule for all words; patterns must be learned.

Example: كِتَابٌ — “a book”

SingularDualBroken plural
كِتَابٌ كِتَابَانِ / كِتَابَيْنِ كُتُبٌ

The same spelling عَيْنٌ can mean “eye” or “fountain”; the plural distinguishes meaning (أَعْيُنٌ vs. عُيُونٌ).

Qurʾānic examples — broken plurals

لَهُمْ قُلُوبٌ لَا يَفْقَهُونَ بِهَا — They have hearts with which they do not understand… (7:179)
أَمْ لَهُمْ أَعْيُنٌ يُبْصِرُونَ بِهَا — Or do they have eyes by which they see…? (7:195)
أَمْ لَهُمْ أَرْجُلٌ يَمْشُونَ بِهَا — Or do they have feet by which they walk…? (7:195)
وَمَا كُنْتَ لَدَيْهِمْ إِذْ يُلْقُونَ أَقْلَامَهُمْ — …You were not with them when they cast their pens… (3:44)
إِنَّمَا أَمْوَالُكُمْ وَأَوْلَادُكُمْ فِتْنَةٌ — Indeed your wealth and your children are but a trial… (8:28)
أَأَرْبَابٌ مُتَفَرِّقُونَ خَيْرٌ — Are many different masters better…? (12:39)
وَيَسْأَلُونَكَ عَنِ الْجِبَالِ — They ask you about the mountains… (20:105)
وَأَزْوَاجُهُ أُمَّهَاتُهُمْ — …And his wives are their mothers… (33:6)
وَالْجُرُوحَ قِصَاصٌ — …And for wounds is legal retribution… (5:45)
وَجَعَلْنَاكُمْ شُعُوبًا وَقَبَائِلَ — …And We made you peoples and tribes… (49:13)
وَإِذْ أَنْتُمْ أَجِنَّةٌ فِي بُطُونِ أُمَّهَاتِكُمْ — …When you were fetuses in your mothers’ wombs… (53:32)

In dual and plural, words may appear in more than one grammatical form (case endings); later lessons spell out those patterns in context. Use the Iʿrāb nodes for rafʿ / naṣb / jarr on full phrases. A body-parts singular/plural vocabulary list (Lesson 16) is under Noun → مُفْرَدَاتُ الْجَسَد.
