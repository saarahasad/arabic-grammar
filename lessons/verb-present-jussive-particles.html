Jussive Particles (Hurūf Jāzimah)

حُرُوفٌ جَازِمَة

Certain particles (حُرُوفٌ جَازِمَة) place the following mudāriʿ in the jussive (مَجْزُوم). In the basic tri-literal pattern, the last vowel is often sukūn (ْ) where the indicative would have damma (ُ). Conjugation shapes match those after لِـ (Lam al-amr) and prohibitive لَا. Jazm shows clearly on singulars and many “light” forms; where the indicative has a pronounced ن (dual / sound masc. plural), that ن is absent in jussive.

Overview — five particles

ParticleTypical senseNote
لِshould / “let”With 1st & 3rd person — see Lam al-amr in the mind map.
لَاdo not / should notProhibition — see Negation under Present.
لَمْdid not / was notNegates the action as never having happened; the following mudāriʿ is read with past meaning.
لَمَّاnot yetBefore mudāriʿ only in this sense; before a past verb it means when (no jussive on the past).
إِنْifConditional use — see Conditional words under Present.
حُرُوفُ الشَّرْط: Vocabulary, شَرْط / جَوَابُ الشَّرْط, Quranic examples for إِنْ, لَوْ, إِلَّا, etc. — open Conditional words in the mind map (under Present).

لَمْ — past meaning on a mudāriʿ shape

لَمْ يَلِدْ وَلَمْ يُولَدْ — He begets not, nor is He begotten (112:3)
أَلَمْ نَجْعَلْ لَهُ عَيْنَيْنِ — Have We not made for him two eyes? (90:8)
أَوَلَمْ يَعْلَمْ أَنَّ اللَّهَ يَعْلَمُ — Did they not know that Allah knows…? (22:70)

لَمَّا + mudāriʿ — “not yet”

وَآخَرِينَ مِنْهُمْ لَمَّا يَلْحَقُوا بِهِمْ — …and others of them who have not yet joined them (62:3)
وَلَمَّا يَدْخُلِ الْإِيمَانُ فِي قُلُوبِكُمْ — …while faith has not yet entered your hearts (49:14)
وَلَمَّا يَعْلَمْ الَّذِينَ جَاهَدُوا مِنكُمْ — …and Allah has not yet made known… (3:142)
Phonetics: A final sukūn may be read as kasra (ِ) when the next word begins with هَمْزَةُ الْوَصْل, for smoother connection.

لَمَّا + past — “when”

Here لَمَّا does not jussive the verb; the past tense keeps its normal endings.

فَلَمَّا وَضَعَتْهَا — So when she delivered her… (3:36)
فَلَمَّا فَصَلَ طَالُوتُ بِالْجُنُودِ — When Talut set forth with the armies… (2:249)
فَلَمَّا رَأَى الشَّمْسَ بَازِغَةً — When he saw the sun rising… (6:78)

“Hidden” إِنْ after a command

After an imperative, the next mudāriʿ may be jussive as if إِنْ (“if”) were understood: “Do X — (if you do,) Y will happen.”

وَأَدْخِلْ يَدَكَ فِي جَيْبِكَ تَخْرُجْ بَيْضَاءَ مِنْ غَيْرِ سُوءٍ — Put your hand into your bosom; it will come forth white… (27:12)
تَعَالَوْا يَسْتَغْفِرْ لَكُمْ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ — Come; Allah’s Messenger will ask forgiveness for you (63:5)
قُلْ تَعَالَوْا أَتْلُ مَا حَرَّمَ رَبُّكُمْ عَلَيْكُمْ — Say: Come, I will recite what your Lord has forbidden… (6:151)
اتَّقُوا اللَّهَ وَقُولُوا قَوْلًا سَدِيدًا يُصْلِحْ لَكُمْ أَعْمَالَكُمْ — …fear Allah and speak a straight word — that He may set right your deeds… (33:70–71)
