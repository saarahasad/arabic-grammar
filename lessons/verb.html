The Verb

الْفِعْل

The verb (الْفِعْل) expresses an action or state, attributed to people or things in the past, present, or future.

Markers of a verb (عَلَامَاتُ الْفِعْل)

  • Particles before the verb — e.g. قَدْ, لَنْ, لَمْ, سَوْفَ, سَ (the following word is read as a verb in the right tense).
  • Present-tense prefixes — one of the four letters of أَنِيت (أَ، نَ، يَ، تَ) at the beginning of the mudāriʿ, e.g. يَفْعَلُ, تَفْعَلُ.
  • Suffixes for the doer — past tense adds endings for “who did it”: e.g. فَعَلْتُ (I), فَعَلْنَا (we), فَعَلْتَ (you m.); feminine singular doer often shows تْ as in فَعَلَتْ (she).

Most Arabic verbs are built from three root letters (a “tri-literal” verb). A sound, regular verb of this kind is called الْفِعْلُ السَّالِمُ (al-fiʿl al-sālim).

Grammar often illustrates patterns with the letters ف–ع–ل (fa ʿain lam): the first root letter stands for ف, the middle for ع, the last for ل, as in the model فَعَلَ (“to do”).
