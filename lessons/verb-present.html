Present & Future Tense

الْمُضَارِع

The mudāriʿ (الْمُضَارِع) describes the present or future in the same form; context and particles (like سَ or سَوْفَ) tell you if the meaning is “now” or “will.”

After the prefix (one of أَنِيت), a sound tri-literal mudāriʿ often has: fatḥah on the first root letter, sukūn on the second, ḍammah on the last — while the middle root letter may carry various vowels (e.g. يَفْعُلُ vs يَفْعَلُ vs يَفْعِلُ).

“Signs” of the mudāriʿ — prefixes

Four letters begin the mudāriʿ (with fatha on the following letter in sound tri-literal verbs): ي، ت، أ، ن — e.g. يَفْعَلُ، تَفْعَلُ، أَفْعَلُ، نَفْعَلُ.

Suffixes (dual / plural)

انِ (dual), ونَ (masc. plural), نَ (fem. plural and some other forms). There is no dual suffix for 1st person.

Rafʿ on the mudāriʿ (indicative)

When the verb is in the indicative (مَرْفُوع): if there is no plural/dual suffix, the sign is usually damma on the last letter; if there is a suffix, ثُبُوتُ النُّونِ (the pronounced nūn) shows rafʿ for the “five verbs” pattern.

Future markers

سَ or سَوْفَ before the mudāriʿ often limit the meaning to future (“will / shall”).

سَوْفَ تَعَلْمُونَ — Soon you will know (74:3)
سَيَصْلَوْنَ نَارًا — They will enter the Fire (future with سَ)

More examples (mudāriʿ in context)

يَفْعَلُ اللَّهُ مَا يَشَاءُ — Allah does what He wills (14:27)
تَفْعَلُونَ — you (all) do (16:91)
لِمَ تَكْفُرُونَ بِآيَاتِ اللَّهِ — Why do you reject…? (3:98)
Remember: The same mudāriʿ form can be present or future; particles and sentence context disambiguate.
