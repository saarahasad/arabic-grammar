Naʿt — Adjective

النَّعْت

An-naʿt (النَّعْت) is one kind of tābiʿ (follower): the adjective that describes a noun. It is also called ṣifah (صِفَة). The noun it describes is the manʿūt (الْمَنْعُوت) or mawṣūf (الْمَوْصُوف). For the general idea of followers, see Followers (التوابع) in the mind map.

The naʿt is a follower: it agrees with the mawṣūf in:

  • Case — rafʿ, naṣb, and jarr (رَفْعُهُ وَنَصْبُهُ وَخَفْضُهُ).
  • Definiteness — definite vs. indefinite (تَعْرِيفُهُ وَتَنْكِيرُهُ).
  • Number — singular, dual, plural (إِفْرَادُهُ وَتَثْنِيَتُهُ وَجَمْعُهُ).
  • Gender — masculine vs. feminine (تَذْكِيرُهُ وَتَأْنِيثُهُ).

Word order

Unlike English, the adjective follows the noun it describes: “the big house” → الْبَيْتُ الْكَبِيرُ (al-baytu l-kabīru) — literally “the house the big.”

Examples — definiteness and gender

IndefiniteDefiniteMeaning
Masc.قَلَمٌ جَدِيدٌالْقَلَمُ الْجَدِيدُa / the new pen
Fem.سَيَّارَةٌ جَمِيلَةٌالسَّيَّارَةُ الْجَمِيلَةُa / the beautiful car

In the indefinite row, both noun and adjective show tanwīn; in the definite row, both take الْ and drop tanwīn on the last letter.
