The noun

الْإِسْم

The noun (الْإِسْم, al-ism) names a person, place, thing, or sometimes an action understood as a “thing.”

Sample word

كِتَابٌ

“a book” — indefinite nominative with tanwīn

Signs that a word is a noun عَلَامَاتُ الْإِسْم

At an introductory level, Arabic often marks or situates nouns in these ways. You only need one clue; they often appear together.

1

Tanwīn — indefinite endings

تَنْوِين

Double vowel signs on the last letter: ٌ · ٍ · ً. Example: كِتَابٌ.

2

The definite article

الْ

Prefixed to make the noun definite, e.g. الْكِتَابُ.

3

Kasrah on the last letter

Common after prepositions like مِنْ, in construct (إِضَافَة), and in other noun states you will study under Iʿrāb.

4

Tāʾ marbūṭah

ة

Many feminine nouns end in ة (with important exceptions you will meet later).

5

After a preposition

حَرْفُ جَرٍّ

The word after a preposition is usually a noun in the genitive (مَجْرُور), e.g. فِي الْبَيْتِ.

6

Word classes used like nouns

أَسْمَاءُ الْإِشَارَة (demonstratives), الضَّمَائِر (pronouns), الْأَسْمَاءُ الْمَوْصُولَة (e.g. الَّذِي).
